The 30-day rollout guide.

A week-by-week plan for getting AI Safe@Work in front of every relevant person in your organisation — or, if you are an MSP, across multiple customers in parallel. Designed to leave behind an audit-grade evidence trail when day 30 closes.

Print landscape for the tables. File alongside training register + AUP signature pack.
Who this is for

Two audiences, one playbook

Manager · internal rollout

You are responsible for AI literacy in your own organisation. Your job over the next 30 days is to get every relevant person trained, signed-up to the AUP, and recorded in the training register, with a route to escalate when something goes wrong.

Read every section. Skip the purple boxes.

MSP partner · multi-customer rollout

You are responsible for rolling AI Safe@Work out across multiple SMB customers under your managed-service contract. Your job is to deliver consistent governance posture at every customer, in parallel, with a per-customer audit trail you can hand back.

Read every section. The purple boxes are yours. Read the MSP / IT Admin track first if you haven't.

What "rollout" actually means

Rollout is the controlled introduction of: (a) the AI Safe@Work training material, (b) an Acceptable Use Policy that staff sign, (c) a training register that records who has done what, and (d) an escalation route for incidents. You are not deploying software. You are deploying governance.

This guide assumes the underlying AI tools (ChatGPT Enterprise, Microsoft Copilot for M365, Google Workspace AI, Claude for Work, or none of the above) are already in scope for the organisation, or that the rollout is part of approving them in the first place. If neither is true yet, run a vendor-due-diligence pass using the vendor questionnaire first.

MSP variant — role mapping

You will play the rollout lead role at every customer in parallel. You will need a customer-side counterpart per customer (their COO, Office Manager, or HR lead) to hold the "sponsor" hat and chase non-completion. Block 4 hours per customer in week 1; 1 hour/week thereafter. For Strategic-tier customers, plan a kick-off call jointly with the customer's named champion.

Week 0 — preparation (the 3 days before kickoff)

Week 0 · 3 days

Get the boring decisions out of the way.

Goal: zero open questions on day 1 of week 1.

  • Name every person in the role table above. Get sponsor agreement in writing (Slack thread is fine; email is better).
  • Decide the audience scope. Default: every employee who uses email. Note exclusions and why.
  • Decide whether managers must complete the Manager Portal too. Default: yes.
  • Confirm the approved-tool list (or decide to ship a one-tool list now and expand later). Reference Module 4.
  • Open the training register for the rollout. Add a row per person, prefilled with name, role, department.
  • Draft the rollout-kickoff message from the sponsor (template below in week 1).
  • Customise the AUP template — fill bracketed sections, get DPO and legal review where required.
  • Set up incident channel — name it AISW-incidents or similar; brief on-call routing in Module 9.
  • If you are a high-risk AI deployer under the EU AI Act: confirm whether FRIA is required and start it in parallel.
  • If personal data is involved: confirm whether DPIA is needed under GDPR Art 35.
MSP variant — week 0

Customers in cohorts of 3–5, not 1-by-1 or all-at-once. Set up one training register per customer (separate spreadsheet / HRIS tab); never co-mingle customer rolls. Brand the AUP per customer — your customers do not want to send a contract that says "AI Safe@Work" at the top. Confirm with each customer's sponsor whether their incident escalation goes via your service desk first, or direct to their internal owner.

Week 1 — launch and kickoff

Week 1 · 5 working days

The all-hands moment. Make it real.

Goal: every person in scope has read the kickoff message, knows where the course lives, and has started Module 1.

  • Day 1, morning: sponsor sends the kickoff message (template in section "Sample kickoff message" below).
  • Day 1, mid-morning: rollout lead posts a follow-up in the team channel with the direct link, the AUP for signature, and the deadline.
  • Day 1, afternoon: hold a 30-minute live kickoff call. Walk the agenda + show the cert at the end so people see what they're working toward.
  • Day 2: line managers confirm with each direct report that they have started Module 1 or have a date booked to start.
  • Day 3–5: rollout lead posts a daily completion-count update in the team channel. Not who hasn't done it. Just the count.
  • End of week 1: rollout lead has at least 30% of staff at Module 5 or beyond. If this is below 20%, escalate; the rollout is being silently deprioritised.
  • End of week 1: the training register is being filled in row-by-row by the rollout lead as completions land.
MSP variant — week 1

Stagger customer kickoffs across the week to spread Q&A load on your service desk — Monday + Wednesday + Friday for three customers is better than three on Monday. Pre-write per-customer kickoff messages from the customer's own sponsor — they do not have time to draft them. Bring the customer's IT lead into your MSP Slack for the duration so questions don't bounce through email.

Sample kickoff message

From the sponsor. Edit, but do not stretch — short messages outperform long ones in week-1 reads.

Subject: AI at work — what we're rolling out this month

We use AI tools in our work, and the rules around them have caught up with reality. The EU AI Act now requires us to make sure everyone using AI knows what they're doing — not just IT, everyone.

We're rolling out a short, free, plain-English course this month. It's 11 modules, about 90 minutes total. You can do it on your phone in the queue at lunch.

What I'd like you to do:
1. Start with Module 1 this week.
2. Finish all 11 modules and the assessment by [date — 21 days from now].
3. Sign the attached Acceptable Use Policy and email it back to [HR / rollout lead].

The course is here: [link]. The AUP is attached. Questions go to [name].

This is the most useful 90 minutes you'll spend this quarter. Genuinely.

— [Sponsor name]

Week 2 — completion drive

Week 2 · 5 working days

The middle. Where rollouts go to die. Don't let it.

Goal: 70%+ of staff complete the course; non-completers identified by name.

  • Day 1: rollout lead pulls a completion snapshot. Identifies the people stuck on Module 1.
  • Day 2: line managers have a one-line check-in with stuck team members. "Anything blocking you?" Listen.
  • Day 3: champion(s) host an optional drop-in Q&A session. Keep it 30 minutes. Record questions and feed them to the rollout lead.
  • Day 4: midweek update from sponsor in the team channel. Two lines. Completion count + thanks to early finishers by name.
  • Day 5: rollout lead emails the line manager of anyone still below Module 4. Asks for a date.
  • Through the week: AUP signatures collected in parallel. Do not gate course completion behind AUP signature — they can flow in either order.
MSP variant — week 2

Customer-side sponsor fatigue starts here. Send each customer sponsor a weekly two-line update: "{Customer X}: 14/22 completed, ahead of schedule. Two flagged for follow-up by line managers." This protects them from looking at a spreadsheet and protects you from chasing every individual.

Week 3 — assessment and certification

Week 3 · 5 working days

The actual evidence appears. Catch it.

Goal: 90%+ have completed Module 11 (final assessment) at ≥ 80%, certificate referenced in training register.

  • Day 1: rollout lead pulls list of who has passed Module 11. For each pass, copy the certificate reference (AISW-MNN-YYMMDD-XXXX) into the training register.
  • Day 1–2: people who failed Module 11 first time retake the modules where they got the question wrong. (The course shows them which.) This is fine. Re-tests do not require special handling.
  • Day 3: AUP signatures should be at ≥ 90%. Chase the rest. Final round of signatures on day 5 cutoff.
  • Day 4: rollout lead writes a one-page completion summary for the sponsor and DPO. Numbers + named outliers + plan for them.
  • Day 5: DPO signs off the training register section for this rollout cycle. Filed.
MSP variant — week 3

Per-customer completion summary lands as a one-page report. Copy this exact format: "Customer: X. Population: 22. Completed: 21 (95%). Average score: 87%. Outliers: 1 (Y. Reason: extended leave. Recovery date: [date]). AUP signatures: 22/22. Incident channel: live." Customer sponsors love this format because it is the answer to "are we compliant" in three minutes.

Week 4 — embed and handover

Week 4 · 5 working days

Make it routine, not a project. Then close the project.

Goal: incident channel proven, refresh date booked, audit binder closed.

  • Day 1: rollout lead runs a tabletop incident drill. Pick a scenario from Module 9. Walk the escalation. Time it.
  • Day 2: rollout lead confirms quarterly refresh cadence in shared calendar. Refresh = re-issue the changelog, run AUP restatement, re-attest training register.
  • Day 3: rollout lead compiles audit binder. Contents: signed AUP pack · training register snapshot · completion summary · DPO sign-off · incident channel screenshot · this rollout guide marked complete.
  • Day 4: rollout lead does a 30-minute handover with sponsor: what's running now, what to watch, what the quarterly cadence looks like.
  • Day 5: close the project. Send the close-out message. The rollout lead role downshifts to "register owner" — 30 minutes a month, not 4 hours a week.
MSP variant — week 4

Customer handover is your moment of differentiation. Hand the audit binder back as a PDF + the original training register editable file. Tell the customer what you keep on their behalf: the rollout guide marked complete, the incident channel runbook, the date of the next quarterly refresh. Bill the customer for the rollout if your contract calls for it. Add this customer to your quarterly QBR roll.

Artefacts you should have at the end of 30 days

  1. Signed Acceptable Use Policy from every person in scope (template).
  2. Completed training register (template), DPO sign-off applied.
  3. Completion summary one-pager (see week 3).
  4. Incident channel proven via a tabletop drill, runbook filed (Module 9 as reference).
  5. Quarterly refresh date booked in shared calendar.
  6. This rollout guide marked complete with the dates of each week.
  7. Where applicable: signed-off DPIA and / or FRIA.
  8. Audit binder ZIP / folder containing everything above.

Standards mapping

  • EU AI Act — Article 4 (literacy obligation, evidenced by training register + completion summary); Article 14 (human oversight, embedded via the Manager Portal); Article 26 (deployer obligations, supported by AUP + incident channel + tabletop drill); Article 27 (FRIA where applicable); Article 50 (transparency, addressed in the AUP).
  • GDPR — Article 5 principles (covered in AUP); Article 32 (security, supported by approved-tool list); Article 35 DPIA (where personal data is processed); Article 39 DPO awareness duty (DPO sign-off on register).
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Clauses 7.2 (competence), 7.3 (awareness), 7.4 (communication); Annex A.4.5 (resources); A.6.1.2 (impact assessment); A.6.2.6 (operational incident handling).
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Clause 7.3 (awareness); Annex A.6.3 (training); A.5.10 (acceptable use of information and other associated assets); A.5.24–A.5.28 (incident management).
  • NIST AI RMF 1.0 — Govern 4 (workforce diversity, equity, inclusion, accessibility, and culture); Govern 6 (policies and procedures).

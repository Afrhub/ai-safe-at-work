Security & AI-crawler posture · v2026.05

What we do to keep your data, and ours, safe.

A short statement of what AI Safe@Work does at the edge, in the browser and in the audit pack to (a) protect customer data, (b) signal opt-out from AI model training and (c) keep the free course citable by the AI tools that link back to source. Hand it to a procurement team in five minutes.

§
In one sentence

Training crawlers are blocked everywhere. Citation / answer-engine crawlers are welcome on the free course (they cite with a link, which drives the trust signal). Paid surfaces are auth-walled, served with X-Robots-Tag: noai, noimageai, and excluded from every machine-readable opt-out file.

Why we split AI crawlers into two classes

Most vendor "AI bots" are actually two separate things under different user-agents. One ingests content for model training and gives nothing back. The other fetches at query time, cites the source, and drives a click. We treat them differently, same vendor or not.

For paid surfaces (when published), all classes are blocked, citation or training. Auth-wall is primary defence; everything else is layered.

Layered defence, six controls

  1. robots.txt, honour-system but catches every well-behaved crawler. Training UAs blocked site-wide; citation UAs blocked on paid paths only.
  2. tdm-policy.json (TDMRep), machine-readable EU CDSM Art 4 opt-out. Required for legal weight in EU.
  3. HTTP headers, X-Robots-Tag: noai, noimageai, nosnippet on every paid response (Bing + Adobe + others honour). Strict-Transport-Security, Content-Security-Policy, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy, Permissions-Policy site-wide.
  4. Edge bot management, TLS / headless / behavioural fingerprinting on paid paths (catches UA spoofing that robots.txt cannot). Activates once paid surfaces ship.
  5. Auth-wall, per-account rate limits, session-behaviour anomaly detection, on every commercially-valuable surface.
  6. Honeypots + canary content, embedded tokens in paid exports and certificates; monthly web-search for canary strings to detect leaks.

What we don't do

  • We don't track free-course readers. There's no analytics, no advertising pixels, no behavioural-tracking cookie.
  • We don't ingest learner content into AI training pipelines, ours or anyone else's.
  • We don't block citation engines on the free course. The wedge depends on AI tools being able to cite our content back to readers with a link.
  • We don't promise crawler blocking against state-actor-grade scrapers, that's beyond any commercial-tier control.

Procurement teams

Hand this page (or the Standards Map) to your DPO / compliance lead. If you need a vendor-questionnaire response file, the structure mirrors the AI vendor diligence questionnaire we publish for use against other vendors, we hold ourselves to the same questions.

Review cadence

Posture re-walked quarterly per changelog. Vendor UA policies change; the classification table above is verified each quarter and updated if the line moves (e.g. if PerplexityBot starts training, it moves to the block-everywhere class). Verification record: .audit/security/anti-scraping-verification-YYYY-QN.md.

If you have a security concern, a leak, an exploit, an open canary hit, please report via the complaints route. We aim to respond within five working days; sooner for active leaks.

Corrections & concerns

Tell us what's wrong.

A course about not making mistakes should not make them either. If you have found an error, a broken link, an accessibility barrier or a passage that is misleading or out of date, please tell us. We respond within five working days.

How to report

Until the dedicated contact channel is live, please report via the channel you obtained the course from. We will publish a direct email address and an issue tracker here as soon as the production domain is provisioned.

What to include

  1. The page URL where you saw the issue.
  2. What you observed (the wrong text, the broken link, the accessibility barrier).
  3. What you expected to see.
  4. If you can share it: the source you believe is correct (link to the regulator, court filing, standard, etc.).
  5. If accessibility-related: which assistive technology and which device / browser.

How we handle reports

  1. Acknowledge, within five working days.
  2. Triage, categorise as factual correction / broken link / accessibility / safety / other.
  3. Investigate, for factual corrections, we check the primary source ourselves.
  4. Fix, for clear factual errors, broken links, or accessibility barriers, we publish the correction as soon as practicable and log it in the changelog.
  5. Tell you, when the fix is live, or when we have a substantive response.

If you disagree with our response

Tell us. We will reconsider. If you have a substantive concern about how we handle personal data, you also have the right to raise a complaint with your national data-protection supervisory authority (ICO in the UK, CNIL in France, equivalent body in your member state).

Where this fits in our process

Reports submitted here feed into our quarterly content refresh cycle. Material errors are fixed sooner; minor corrections batch with the next quarterly release. See the changelog for past correction entries.

i
A note on tone

If you write to us angry, you'll still get a polite reply on the same SLA. We'd rather know than not know. The course exists to be useful; if it isn't, we want to fix it.

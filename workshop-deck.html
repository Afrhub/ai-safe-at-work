Partner sales conversation + customer kickoff

The MSP workshop deck.

Ten slides for an MSP partner to use in their customer sales conversation, their customer kickoff session, or a procurement-team intro. Each slide carries a talk-track for the presenter. Designed to print A4 landscape one-slide-per-page or to run on-screen.

Print landscape one-per-page. Edit the customer name + sector references before sending.
Slide 1 of 10

Why this conversation, why now

Cold-open slide. Two minutes.
  • AI is now used by your staff every day, Outlook, Teams, summaries, drafts, code suggestions.
  • EU AI Act Article 4 (in force February 2025) requires evidence of AI literacy for staff.
  • GDPR + sector regulators (FCA, MHRA, CQC, NHS DSPT, EHRC) have all extended scrutiny to AI use.
  • Your customers, your auditor, and the regulator will all ask: "show us how you govern this."
  • The answer cannot be "we don't" or "we will".
Talk-track Open with the urgency. Use the regulator that matters most to this customer, FCA if FS, CQC if healthcare, ICO + EHRC if local authority. The point is not to scare; it's to acknowledge the conversation is overdue. Say "we have a 30-day plan to fix this. Let me show you."
Slide 2 of 10

What AI governance actually means

De-mystify the term.
  • Staff know what AI tools they can use, and which they can't, why, and on what data.
  • Those staff are trained + recorded as trained, Article 4 evidence.
  • The AI tools have been vendor-checked, DPA + sub-processor + residency + change-notification.
  • Decisions made with AI have a named human owner, Article 14 oversight, Article 22 GDPR safeguards.
  • Incidents have an escalation route, practiced, not just written.
  • Audit can find the binder, AUP signed, register filled, FRIA / DPIA where needed, ATRS where public-sector.
Talk-track Don't list every regulation; pick the three that matter most to this customer. The point is governance = "staff know what to do + we can show evidence." That's it. Most customers expect this to be much more complicated than it is.

Show the estate. One minute.
  • 11 universal modules, plain-English, EU-first, ~90 minutes total.
  • 6 Relevant Roles, Manager · DPO · MSP / IT Admin · Microsoft Copilot · Shadow AI · Governance & Procurement.
  • 3 sector overlays, Financial Services · Healthcare · Public Sector.
  • 7 governance templates, AUP, vendor questionnaire, training register, DPIA, FRIA, incident form, ATRS.
  • 30-day rollout guide, week-by-week plan with KPIs, kickoff message, pitfall list.
  • SCORM 1.2 + xAPI export, drops into any LMS your customer already has.
  • EU-locale shells (FR + DE), for multi-jurisdiction staff.
Talk-track You don't need to walk every item. Pick what matters, for FS, mention the FS overlay + the vendor questionnaire. For healthcare, the healthcare overlay + DCB0160 + ATRS. The platform is broad on purpose; show the customer the bits that match their pain.
Slide 4 of 10

What we deliver, the rollout

The 30-day playbook is the product.
  • Week 0, preparation: name the sponsor + DPO + line managers; customise AUP; open training register.
  • Week 1, launch: sponsor kickoff message; all staff start Module 1; daily completion-count update.
  • Week 2, completion drive: line-manager check-ins; champion drop-in Q&A; 70% target.
  • Week 3, assessment + cert: 90% pass at ≥ 80%; certificate references captured to register.
  • Week 4, embed + handover: tabletop incident drill; quarterly refresh booked; audit binder closed.
Talk-track Walk through the weeks. The customer hears "this is a project with a deliverable, not a programme that never ends." Reference the rollout guide URL, that's the linear playbook the MSP will run. Mention that the audit binder is the artefact they'll show their regulator.
Slide 5 of 10

What you (the customer) need to do

Set realistic expectations.
  • Name a sponsor, CEO, COO, Practice Manager. In writing. They sign first.
  • Name a rollout lead, usually the office manager or compliance lead. 4-6 hrs week 1, 2 hrs/wk thereafter.
  • 30 days of staff time, ~2 hours each, spread across 4 weeks. Mostly self-paced.
  • Tooling decisions, your IT lead names the approved tools (M365 Copilot? ChatGPT Enterprise? Other?). We help if needed.
  • Sign the AUP, every staff member, including contractors.
  • Show up to a 30-minute tabletop drill, your incident lead practises before the real thing.
Talk-track Customers procure governance and expect us to deliver it without them. We won't. Set expectations: 30 days of their attention is the buy-in. We provide everything else. Tell them "if you can give us a sponsor + a rollout lead + 30 days, we have an audit binder at the end of it. If we can't get a sponsor, this won't work and we'll tell you that upfront."

Slide 8 of 10

Common questions, honest answers

Pre-empt objections.
  • "Is this a quick fix?" No. 30 days of focused work + quarterly refresh in perpetuity. We tell you that upfront.
  • "Will my staff sit through it?" 90 minutes of content; mobile-first; the rollout guide is built around getting completion. Yes.
  • "What if we already use M365 Copilot?" The Microsoft Copilot track is specifically for you. It covers governance, audit, incident response for Copilot.
  • "What if a regulator inspects us?" The audit binder is built for that. ATRS for public sector; DCB0160 for healthcare; DORA Art 13 for FS.
  • "What about updates to the EU AI Act?" Quarterly content refresh tracks regulator publications. Public changelog with RSS.
  • "Can we white-label?" Tier 3 Strategic Partner: yes via Schedule D. Tier 2 Growth: co-branded ("Delivered by [MSP] · Powered by AI Safe@Work").
Talk-track Use the questions that match your customer's expected concerns. If they're regulated heavily, lean on the audit-binder answer. If they're price-conscious, lean on "30 days of work, then quarterly refresh, not infinite consulting."

What happens next

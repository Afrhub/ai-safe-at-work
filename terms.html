A short, plain-language statement of what this course covers and what it does not.

This is general guidance, not legal advice

The course explains GDPR, the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 42001 in plain language for non-technical readers. It is general educational material. It is not a substitute for legal advice from a qualified lawyer in your jurisdiction, or for the specific compliance advice your business may need.

If you have a real legal or compliance question, particularly about a specific incident, a specific contract, or a specific regulatory obligation, talk to a qualified professional.

The standards keep moving

The EU AI Act is being phased in over 2025-2027. Case law on AI and copyright is evolving every few months. Guidance from supervisory authorities changes. Specific articles, deadlines, and amounts cited in this course were accurate at the time of writing in 2026, but you should check primary sources before acting on them.

Use of the material

The content of this course is free to read and free to share with colleagues. Don't strip the source acknowledgements. Don't pass it off as your own work. If you'd like to use it as part of your business's internal training, that's the intent, go ahead.

No warranties

The course is provided "as is". Following its guidance won't, by itself, make any specific business compliant with any specific obligation, compliance is always context-specific. We don't accept liability for losses resulting from acting on the course's content.

Contact

The course is intentionally a static resource. If you found something wrong or unclear, raise it through whichever channel you got the course from.

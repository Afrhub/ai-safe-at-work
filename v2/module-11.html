AI governance is now part of modern workplace responsibility

Throughout this course you have learned how AI tools process information, why governance matters, how to reduce operational risk, how to identify unsafe usage and why human oversight remains essential.

The purpose of this assessment is not to catch people out, discourage AI usage or create fear around technology. The objective is to confirm employees can use AI more safely, recognise governance boundaries and apply responsible decision-making in day-to-day work.

Assessment overview

  • 5 scenario-based questions
  • 3 operational judgement questions
  • Suggested pass mark: 80% (7 of 8)

Scenario 1 — Customer information

A support employee wants to use AI to summarise a customer complaint email. The email contains names, account references and sensitive customer information. The employee plans to use a free public AI chatbot.

What should the employee do?

  1. Use the tool because it improves efficiency.
  2. Remove sensitive information and use an approved enterprise AI platform if organisational policy allows.
  3. Upload the email without changes because the AI provider is well known.
  4. Share the email from a personal account instead.
Show correct answer B — remove sensitive information and use an approved enterprise AI platform if organisational policy allows.

Scenario 2 — AI-generated report

A manager uses AI to generate a business summary for leadership. The AI output looks professional, includes statistics and provides operational recommendations. The manager has not verified the information.

What should happen next?

  1. Send the report immediately because the AI sounds confident.
  2. Verify important information, review the recommendations and apply human oversight.
  3. Assume the AI checked the information automatically.
  4. Remove any mention that AI was used.
Show correct answer B — verify important information, review the recommendations and apply human oversight.

Scenario 3 — Suspicious request

An employee receives an urgent Teams message appearing to come from a senior executive requesting immediate payment approval. The message sounds realistic, references genuine projects and pressures fast action.

What is the safest response?

  1. Approve immediately due to urgency.
  2. Verify using another approved communication channel and follow organisational process.
  3. Ignore approval procedures because the request appears genuine.
  4. Forward banking details without verification.
Show correct answer B — verify using another approved communication channel and follow organisational process.

Scenario 4 — AI plugin discovery

An employee discovers a browser extension automatically sending webpage content to an external AI service. No governance approval exists.

What should the employee do?

  1. Ignore it because no issue has happened yet.
  2. Continue using it because it saves time.
  3. Escalate the issue according to organisational governance or security processes.
  4. Recommend everyone else installs it.
Show correct answer C — escalate the issue according to organisational governance or security processes.

Scenario 5 — Recruitment decision

A hiring manager uses AI to score job candidates automatically. The manager notices the AI consistently ranks certain candidates lower without clear reasoning.

What is the safest approach?

  1. Trust the AI because it improves efficiency.
  2. Review the process critically, apply human oversight and follow organisational recruitment governance.
  3. Allow AI to make the final hiring decision independently.
  4. Ignore fairness concerns because the tool is automated.
Show correct answer B — review the process critically, apply human oversight and follow organisational recruitment governance.

Operational judgement question 1

Which statement is TRUE?

  1. "Enterprise AI removes the need for governance."
  2. "Human oversight remains essential when using AI."
  3. "Professional wording guarantees AI accuracy."
  4. "Unapproved AI usage is acceptable if productivity improves."
Show correct answer B — human oversight remains essential when using AI.

Operational judgement question 2

What is an example of "shadow AI"?

  1. Using approved enterprise AI within policy.
  2. Following governance review processes.
  3. Using unapproved AI tools or personal AI accounts for business activity.
  4. Completing AI awareness training.
Show correct answer C — using unapproved AI tools or personal AI accounts for business activity.

Operational judgement question 3

Which type of information should generally not be entered into unapproved AI systems?

  1. Confidential customer information
  2. Passwords and API keys
  3. Commercial pricing
  4. All of the above
Show correct answer D — all of the above.

Continuing responsibility

Completing this course does not mean AI is risk free, governance is no longer required or human oversight can be removed. Safe AI usage requires ongoing awareness, good judgement, operational governance and responsible behaviour.

Final workplace reminder

Before using AI: pause · think · verify · follow organisational guidance. If unsure, ask before proceeding.

Final key takeaways

  • AI can provide significant productivity and business benefits
  • Governance supports safe and responsible AI adoption
  • Human oversight remains essential
  • Approved tools, verification and escalation reduce operational risk
  • Accountability stays with people and organisations, not the AI system
  • Safe AI usage is a shared organisational responsibility

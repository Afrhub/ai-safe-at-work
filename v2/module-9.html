AI incidents, escalation, and what to do when something goes wrong.

By the end of this module you will
  • Understand what an AI-related incident may look like
  • Recognise when escalation is required
  • Understand the importance of rapid reporting
  • Apply safer behaviours when something goes wrong involving AI

AI incidents are operational incidents.

They may involve:

  • data exposure
  • incorrect outputs
  • governance failures
  • security concerns
  • supplier issues
  • or reputational risk.

Importantly: many AI incidents begin with:

  • misunderstanding
  • poor judgement
  • accidental oversharing
  • or use of unapproved tools.

The goal is not:

"never make mistakes."

The goal is:

  • identify issues quickly
  • reduce impact
  • escalate appropriately.

Early reporting often reduces:

  • operational damage
  • compliance exposure
  • customer impact
  • recovery costs.

What Is An AI Incident?

An AI incident is any event involving AI usage that may create:

  • operational risk
  • governance concerns
  • security exposure
  • reputational damage
  • or compliance issues.

Examples may include:

  • confidential information entered into an unapproved AI tool
  • AI-generated misinformation shared externally
  • AI-assisted phishing or impersonation
  • use of unapproved AI systems
  • AI-generated biased or inappropriate content
  • customer complaints involving AI-generated responses
  • AI-assisted security misconfiguration
  • unauthorised AI plugins or browser extensions

Important Principle

Reporting an issue early is usually safer than hiding it.

Organisations generally prefer:

  • rapid escalation
  • transparency
  • controlled response

rather than delayed discovery.

Real-World Scenario 1

Scenario

"The customer data was pasted accidentally."

An employee realises they pasted customer information into a public AI chatbot while trying to summarise a support issue. The employee initially considers:

"not telling anyone because it was an accident."

Several days later:

  • the issue is discovered during an audit review
  • creating larger governance and operational concerns.

Risks Created

  • delayed response
  • governance failures
  • reduced containment options
  • potential compliance exposure

Key Lesson

Early escalation may significantly reduce organisational risk.

Real-World Scenario 2

Scenario

"The AI-generated response was incorrect."

A customer-facing AI-assisted email contains inaccurate information. The response:

  • was not properly reviewed
  • reaches the customer
  • causes confusion and escalation.

The employee hesitates to report the issue because:

"the mistake already happened."

Risks Created

  • customer dissatisfaction
  • reputational harm
  • operational confusion
  • delayed remediation

Key Lesson

Rapid reporting improves the organisation's ability to:

  • correct errors
  • support customers
  • investigate root causes.

Real-World Scenario 3

Scenario

"Nobody approved the AI plugin."

An employee installs an AI browser extension that automatically processes internal information. The extension:

  • was never reviewed
  • is not approved
  • creates unknown supplier governance risks.

The issue is only discovered months later.

Risks Created

  • unmanaged shadow AI
  • supplier governance concerns
  • operational visibility gaps
  • security uncertainty

Key Lesson

Unapproved AI tooling should be escalated early, even if no incident has yet occurred.

Common Types Of AI Incidents

1. Data Exposure Incidents

Examples:

  • customer information entered into unapproved AI systems
  • confidential information processed externally
  • oversharing through enterprise AI platforms

2. Misinformation Incidents

Examples:

  • AI-generated inaccuracies shared externally
  • fabricated references or summaries
  • incorrect operational guidance

3. Governance Violations

Examples:

  • unapproved AI usage
  • bypassing approval processes
  • unauthorised plugins or SaaS tools

4. Security Incidents

Examples:

  • AI-assisted phishing
  • credential exposure
  • infrastructure information entered into AI systems

5. Reputational Incidents

Examples:

  • biased AI-generated content
  • inappropriate outputs
  • customer complaints linked to AI usage

When To Escalate

Escalation may be appropriate if:

  • sensitive information was entered into AI
  • customer impact exists
  • the output appears incorrect or harmful
  • an unapproved AI tool is discovered
  • security concerns exist
  • or organisational policy may have been breached.

If unsure:

escalate and ask.

What Good Escalation Looks Like

Good escalation is:

  • prompt
  • factual
  • transparent
  • calm.

The objective is:

  • containment
  • risk reduction
  • appropriate response.

What To Include In An Escalation

Where possible include:

  • what happened
  • what AI tool was involved
  • what information may have been affected
  • when it occurred
  • who has been informed
  • any immediate containment actions already taken.

Do not:

  • hide information
  • attempt to quietly delete evidence
  • or continue using the affected system without guidance.

Human Behaviour Matters

Many incidents become worse because:

  • employees panic
  • delay reporting
  • or try to fix issues alone.

Good governance culture encourages:

  • transparency
  • escalation
  • learning.

Red / Amber / Green Exercise

GREEN — Good Incident Behaviour

  • reporting issues quickly
  • escalating uncertainty
  • following governance process
  • documenting concerns appropriately

AMBER — Requires Immediate Review

  • accidental data entry into AI
  • suspicious AI-generated outputs
  • unapproved AI tooling
  • customer-facing AI errors

Requires:

  • escalation
  • oversight
  • governance review.

RED — High-Risk Behaviour

  • hiding mistakes
  • bypassing escalation
  • deleting evidence
  • continuing unsafe AI usage
  • ignoring governance concerns

Practical Exercise

Scenario

An employee realises they accidentally uploaded commercially sensitive information into a public AI chatbot. They are unsure:

  • whether the information was retained
  • whether organisational policy was breached
  • or whether reporting is necessary.

What should they do?

  • ignore it because it was accidental
  • escalate the issue promptly
  • follow organisational incident procedures
  • provide factual information to the appropriate team

Correct Answer

Everything except: "ignore it because it was accidental."

Quick knowledge check
Which statement is TRUE?
Practical Workplace Rule

If something involving AI:

  • feels wrong
  • appears risky
  • or may impact the organisation

do not ignore it. Escalate early using approved organisational processes.

Key Takeaways

  • AI incidents may involve governance, security, operational or reputational risk.
  • Early escalation usually reduces organisational impact.
  • Transparency and accountability support effective response.
  • Unapproved AI usage may itself represent a governance concern.
  • Good governance culture encourages escalation, not blame.
  • Human behaviour strongly influences incident outcomes.

Module Completion Outcome

You now understand:

  • what AI-related incidents may look like
  • when escalation may be appropriate
  • how early reporting supports operational governance and risk reduction.

Course Overview

This is the full free core: eleven plain-English modules, read in order, in about ninety minutes. Each module ends with a short quiz; finish the core to unlock the printable checklist and generate your completion certificate, then refresh annually as the rules move. Need the governance documents and templates? Those are in the Plus Pack.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the core course, you will be able to:

  • Recognise what AI tools do with what you type, and tell free, paid and enterprise tiers apart.
  • Spot the never-paste categories of data and keep them out of public AI tools.
  • Pick the right tool for the job, and know when to refuse a task and ask IT instead.
  • Verify what an AI tells you, catching hallucinations, fake citations and made-up references with the two-source rule.
  • Recognise AI-powered scams, deepfakes and business email compromise, and verify identity when a message reads as perfect.
  • Watch for bias and fairness problems before they embarrass the business.
  • Handle copyright and IP questions around AI output and other people's content.
  • Keep audit-grade records of who used what, and know when an AI-assisted decision needs a human signature.
  • Respond when something goes wrong: escalate, preserve evidence and meet the 72-hour GDPR breach clock.
  • Run the 60-second pre-submit check before pasting anything into an AI tool.
  • Place EU AI Act, ISO 42001, ISO 27001 and GDPR in plain English, and know who to ask for audit-level depth.

How to use this course

If you have an hour: read in order. Each module builds on the last.

Want the whole course on one printable page? The 60-second checklist is the final tool. Finish every module to unlock it.

If your manager asked you to "do something about AI compliance": start with Module 12, the standards, then come back to Module 3 and Module 9.

If you are the manager: start with the 30-day rollout guide.

Certification

Each module ends with a short knowledge check. Mark every module complete (the page tracks your progress) and the final tool, the 60-second pre-submit checklist, unlocks. Once the core is complete you can generate a completion certificate as a record that you finished the course.

This is a completion certificate only: evidence that a named person worked through the AI Safe@Work core. It is not an accreditation, and it is not ISO certification. The course cites ISO 42001, ISO 27001, GDPR and the EU AI Act where they are relevant, but certifying an organisation against those standards is a separate, audited process handled by your compliance team and an accredited body.

§
A note on the standards

This course is grounded in ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI management systems), ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (information security), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689). We cite them where they're relevant. But this is a practical course for people who use AI tools, not a certification course for auditors. If you need audit-level depth, your compliance team will go deeper.

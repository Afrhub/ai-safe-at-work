Template · AI vendor diligence v1.0

37 questions · ~45 min for vendor to complete

AI vendor diligence.

Send this to any AI vendor before procurement. Most-blocking questions come first. Modelled on the CSA Consensus Assessments Initiative (CAIQ) with the AI Controls Matrix overlay, mapped to the standards a procurement team gets audited against.

Send as PDF. Most vendors prefer that to filling in a web form they don't trust.
Must, blocker if not satisfactory Should, flag for discussion Nice, adds confidence

1 · Identity & scope

GDPR Art 28 · ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.19
  1. Legal entity name, registered address, and corporate group structure for the contracting entity. MustMany AI vendors operate via a US parent and an Irish or Dutch subsidiary. We need to know which entity signs the DPA.
  2. Confirm the AI tool / service to which this questionnaire applies, with version or product tier. Must
  3. List the underlying foundation models used and whether they are first-party or licensed from a third party. MustIf the vendor wraps OpenAI / Anthropic / Google, that's a sub-processor we need disclosed.

2 · Data handling

GDPR Art 5, 6, 28, 44-50 · ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.34, A.8.12
  1. In which countries is customer data processed and stored? List all regions. Must
  2. Are EU-region-only processing options available for our tenant? Must
  3. What customer data is retained, in what form, and for how long? Include prompts, responses, system metadata, embeddings, vector stores, fine-tuning data. Must
  4. Is customer data used to train, fine-tune, or evaluate any model, first-party or sub-processor, for any customer? Confirm contractually. Must"No" with a contract clause is what we want. "No by default with opt-out" is much weaker.
  5. Can customer data be deleted on request, and within what timeframe? Does deletion propagate to backups, logs, and any sub-processor systems? Must
  6. Describe encryption: at rest, in transit, and key-management options. Are customer-managed keys available? Should
  7. List every sub-processor and the role each plays. Confirm notification mechanism when sub-processors change. Must

3 · Legal & contracts

GDPR Art 28 · EU AI Act Art 26
  1. Provide a copy of your Data Processing Agreement (DPA), including standard contractual clauses for international transfers. Must
  2. Do you offer contractual indemnification against intellectual-property infringement arising from model output? ShouldA handful of vendors do (Microsoft, Adobe, Google, IBM, Anthropic enterprise). Lack of indemnification means we carry the risk.
  3. Under EU AI Act Article 26, will you provide the deployer-facing instructions and information required for our use of the system? Must
  4. Describe your exit and data-portability provisions. Should

4 · Security posture

ISO/IEC 27001 · SOC 2 · NIST CSF
  1. Provide your current ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certificate and Statement of Applicability. Must
  2. Provide your most recent SOC 2 Type II report (under NDA acceptable). Should
  3. List other relevant attestations, ISO/IEC 27018 (cloud PII), ISO/IEC 42001 (AIMS), ISO/IEC 27701 (PIMS), HIPAA, FedRAMP, IRAP, C5, etc. Nice
  4. Confirm penetration testing cadence and provide a recent summary or attestation. Should
  5. Describe authentication options: SAML/OIDC SSO, SCIM provisioning, MFA enforcement, role-based access controls. Must
  6. Describe logging and audit-trail capabilities exposed to customers. Should

5 · AI-specific risk

EU AI Act · NIST AI RMF · OWASP LLM Top 10 · CSA AICM
  1. For each model used, declare provider, version, intended-purpose statement, and known limitations. Must
  2. Is any model classified as high-risk under EU AI Act Annex III for any reasonable use case? If yes, confirm Article 13 deployer-information will be provided. Must
  3. Describe controls against prompt injection (OWASP LLM01) and improper output handling (LLM05). Should
  4. Describe controls against sensitive-information disclosure (OWASP LLM02), including any cross-tenant isolation guarantees. Must
  5. Describe bias-, fairness-, and safety-evaluation processes for the models behind the product. Frequency. Publicly available results. Should
  6. Describe content-safety filters, jailbreak resistance, and red-teaming activity. Should
  7. Are model outputs watermarked or otherwise machine-detectable for compliance with EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations? Should

6 · Incident response

GDPR Art 33-34 · ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.24-26 · EU AI Act Art 73
  1. What is your contractual SLA for notifying us of a personal-data breach affecting our tenant? MustAnything more than 24 hours is problematic, we have 72 from the moment we have reasonable awareness, and we need time to investigate.
  2. What is your process for notifying us of a serious AI incident under EU AI Act Article 73? Must
  3. Provide a sample incident-notification template you would send to a customer. Should
  4. Where can customers see real-time service status and incident history? Should

7 · Sustainability & conduct

ISO/IEC 42001 A.3 · CSRD where applicable
  1. Disclose energy and water use associated with training and serving the models. Nice
  2. Confirm compliance with data-worker labour standards in any human review / RLHF / red-team operations. Nice
  3. Provide your published AI usage policy and any acceptable-use limits placed on customers. Should

8 · Change & governance

ISO/IEC 42001 Cl 8 · ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.7
  1. Confirm advance-notice period before material changes to the model, the data-handling, the terms of service, or sub-processors. Must
  2. Describe customer-pinning options, can we lock to a specific model version, on a specific date, for a specific period? Should
  3. What is the published end-of-life / deprecation policy for models offered? Should
✓
How to score this
  1. Every Must needs a satisfactory written answer. Any "no" or "we'll get back to you" is a procurement blocker.
  2. Every Should deserves a follow-up conversation if not answered or unclear.
  3. Nice items are confidence-builders, not blockers.
  4. Read the contract / DPA before signing, vendor questionnaires are a representation, but the contract is the obligation.
  5. Re-run this annually with the same vendor. Things change.
§
Where the standards land

GDPR Art 28, processor contracts; Art 5, 32, 44-50, security and transfers; Art 33-34, breach response. EU AI Act Art 13, deployer information; Art 26, deployer obligations; Art 27, FRIA; Art 50, transparency; Art 73, serious incidents. ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.19 (supplier relationships), A.5.23 (cloud), A.5.24-26 (incidents), A.5.34 (PII), A.8.12 (data leakage). ISO/IEC 42001 A.10 (third-party AI relationships). CSA AI Controls Matrix (whole document). OWASP LLM Top 10 (sections 5-7).

AI literacy training register.

The record an EU AI Act Article 4 audit asks for. One row per person, per training event. Designed to be filled in by hand at first, then ported to your HRIS or LMS once you have one.

Print landscape for the table. File alongside HR records under your retention policy (suggest 5 years per audit norms).
Statement of purpose

AI literacy training register for [Company Name]

Effective: [Date] · Version: 1.0 · Owner: [Role, e.g. HR Director / DPO / Head of L&D] · Review cycle: Quarterly · Retention: Five years from end of employment

1. Why this register exists

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) Article 4 places an obligation on providers and deployers of AI systems to ensure, "to their best extent", a sufficient level of AI literacy for staff and other persons dealing with the operation and use of AI systems on their behalf. Article 4 has been in force since 2 February 2025.

An auditor or regulator who asks "how do you discharge your Article 4 obligation" will expect to see (a) an internal training programme, and (b) a record of who has completed it. This register is that record. It also supports ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Clause 7.2 (competence), GDPR Article 39(1)(b) (DPO awareness-raising and training), and ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.6.3 (information security awareness, education and training).

2. Scope

  1. This register covers all employees, contractors, interns and consultants of [Company Name] who in the course of their work use, configure, deploy, instruct, or supervise generative AI tools or AI-enabled features in business software.
  2. It applies regardless of whether the AI tool is company-procured or used on the staff member's personal account for work tasks.
  3. Where a role does not interact with AI at all, that fact is recorded once on row 3 of section 4 ("not applicable").

3. Required cadence

Each person in scope completes:

  1. Onboarding training, before being granted access to AI tools or within their first 30 days, whichever is sooner.
  2. Annual refresh, once per calendar year. The annual refresh restates the never-paste list, the current approved-tool inventory, and any incidents-learned summary from the year.
  3. Role-specific top-up, for managers, DPOs, IT staff, and any role with AI procurement or supervision authority. Cadence: at role change and annually.
  4. Major-change top-up, when the approved-tool inventory changes materially (new enterprise tool, removed tool, change of region, change of contractual terms), within 30 days of the change.

5. What "Course · version" should contain

Enough to reproduce what the learner saw. Examples:

  • AI Safe@Work v2026.05, all 11 modules + 110 MCQs at ≥ 80%, cert AISW-M11-260527-A93F
  • Internal AUP walk-through, recorded session 2026-03-12, attendance logged in Teams
  • Microsoft Copilot vendor onboarding, Microsoft Learn module ID 4f8c, completion 2026-04-01
  • Sector overlay: AI in finance, internal slide deck v2.1, delivered 2026-05-08, signed-off comprehension check

6. What "Certificate ref" should contain

Whatever evidence ties the row to the actual completion event. In order of preference:

  1. The unique certificate identifier issued by the training (e.g. AI Safe@Work issues AISW-MNN-YYMMDD-XXXX).
  2. The SCORM or xAPI completion record from your LMS.
  3. A signed attendance sheet (scanned + filed).
  4. A confirmation email retained in the manager's HR file.
  5. A self-attestation, where the cadence is "annual restatement of the AUP" rather than course completion.

7. Audit-readiness checklist

  1. Every row above is filled with no [brackets] remaining.
  2. Where a learner has not completed by their Next due date, an action is recorded with a date and an owner.
  3. The register is held in a place that is access-controlled and backed up. Spreadsheet on a single laptop is not sufficient.
  4. The retention period is set in line with HR retention (suggested: five years post-employment).
  5. The owner of this register reviews completion quarterly. The review date is recorded below.
  6. For organisations with high-risk AI systems in scope (EU AI Act Annex III), Article 26(7) deployer duties also require role-specific competence; this register flags those roles and links to their additional training records.

8. Standards mapping

  • EU AI Act, Article 4 (AI literacy obligation, in force 2 Feb 2025); Article 26(7) (deployer duty of competent supervision for high-risk systems).
  • GDPR, Article 39(1)(b) (DPO duty to inform and advise; awareness-raising and training of staff); Article 5(1)(f) (integrity and confidentiality, supported by trained staff).
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 7.2 (competence); Clause 7.3 (awareness); Annex A.4.5 (resources, including human resources).
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Annex A.6.3 (information security awareness, education and training).
  • NIST AI RMF 1.0, Govern 4.1 (workforce training on AI risk).

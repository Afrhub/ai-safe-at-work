Role clarity for governance decisions

AI Governance RACI Matrix

Define who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for key AI governance decisions. Clarity prevents confusion and ensures accountability. Aligns with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 5.3.

Post this matrix where the governance steering committee can see it. Review annually or when roles change.
RACI framework

RACI definitions

  • R, Responsible: Does the work; researches the option, prepares the recommendation, implements the decision.
  • A, Accountable: Has final say; approves or rejects the recommendation; owns the outcome if it goes wrong.
  • C, Consulted: Provides input before the decision; asked for advice or perspective; their feedback shapes the recommendation.
  • I, Informed: Told the outcome after the decision is made; needs to know for their own work.

Notes on this matrix

  • Some roles are dual R/A: For example, DPO is both Responsible and Accountable for GDPR compliance decisions. They do the work and own the outcome.
  • Some decisions have multiple Rs: "Respond to incident" has IT and DPO both responsible, they work together.
  • CEO is usually A, not R: The CEO approves final decisions but doesn't do the day-to-day work; others are R/C.
  • Customise to your structure: If you have a separate Risk Officer, move some of the DPO tasks. If IT and Security are the same person, merge those columns.

How to use this matrix

  1. Communicate it: Share with the entire steering committee and all people listed in the columns. They now know their role for each decision type.
  2. Refer to it: When a new AI decision comes up (e.g. "Should we adopt tool X?"), point to the matrix: "Tool approval, DPO is R/A, IT and Procurement are C."
  3. Use it to resolve confusion: If someone asks "Why didn't I get to decide?" you can point to the matrix and explain their role.
  4. Review annually: After a year of governance, review this matrix with your steering committee. Is the R/A/C/I split working or do role changes needed?

Standards referenced

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 5.3 (roles, responsibilities and authorities).

  4. Reference it every time a decision is made: "X is responsible, Y is accountable, Z is consulted."

AI Governance: Executive Brief

A one-page, plain-language summary of AI governance status for the board or executive team. Covers current state, compliance posture, strategic risks, and decisions needed. Aligns with EU AI Act Article 4 (oversight), ISO/IEC 38507 (board oversight of AI), and GDPR Article 5 (accountability).

This is a one-page brief. Use it for board papers, quarterly reviews, or investor meetings.
Executive summary

AI Governance Status: [Quarter / Month YYYY]

Status: AMBER · Governance maturity: Developing · Compliance posture: On track · Key risk: Training completion lag

The situation

[Company Name] uses AI in [10] distinct ways: internal drafting, customer support summarisation, recruitment screening, and invoice categorisation. Staff use [10] approved AI tools (ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude, Copilot, etc.) and an estimated [3] unapproved tools (shadow AI).

Under EU AI Act Article 4 and GDPR Article 5, we have a legal duty to ensure staff are AI-literate, to monitor and manage risks, and to maintain accountability for AI-assisted decisions. This brief covers our current posture.

Compliance status

  • EU AI Act Article 4 (AI literacy): 75% of staff completed AI literacy training; target is 100% by [date]. Managers are lagging: 60% completion.
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments (GDPR Art. 35): 60% of High-risk AI use cases have completed DPIA / FRIA. Four pending; due [dates].
  • Vendor oversight (GDPR Art. 28, EU AI Act Art. 26): All approved tools have Data Processing Agreements in place. No vendor-related compliance breaches this quarter.
  • Policy compliance: One policy breach this month (staff pasted personal data into non-approved tool). Incident investigated; no data loss confirmed. Team re-trained.

Operational risks (top three)

Risk Mitigation Due date
Staff training lag — 25% of staff incomplete Manager accountability; deadline communication; incentive. 2026-06-30
Vendor term changes — ChatGPT model update requires re-assessment DPO review of updated model; compliance memo to stakeholders. 2026-06-20
Incomplete DPIA/FRIA — 4 use cases pending impact assessment Accelerate FRIA completion; interim risk controls in place. 2026-07-31

What we've done

  • Established governance: AI Governance Charter signed by CEO; steering committee formed; quarterly review cycle active.
  • Built registers: AI Tool Register (10 approved + 3 shadow tools tracked); AI Risk Register (12 identified risks, 8 with mitigation plans); AI Use Case Register (7 live use cases).
  • Set standards: AI Acceptable Use Policy (staff trained); vendor questionnaire process (DPA review requirement).
  • Tracked performance: Monthly governance scorecard; zero data-leakage incidents this quarter (down from 1 last quarter).

Decisions needed from the board / leadership

Decision 1: Training deadline

Staff training completion is at 75%. We need a hard deadline and leadership endorsement (e.g. "All staff must complete by June 30"). Recommend: Approve deadline and ask managers to sign off.

Decision 2: Budget for impact assessments

Four High-risk AI use cases are pending GDPR DPIA / EU AI Act FRIA. Completing these internally takes ~2 weeks per use case (5 weeks total). Alternatively, we can hire a specialist (£8,000–£12,000). Recommend: Internal completion if capacity allows; external if time is critical.

Decision 3: Shadow AI governance approach

Three unapproved tools are in use (staff found them independently). We can block them, approve them, or tolerate them with conditions. Recommend: Fast-track approval (5-day review) for tools that meet our criteria; communicate blocklist to staff.

Bottom line

[Company Name] is AMBER on AI governance maturity: we have the right structure (charter, steering committee, registers, policy), but we're lagging on execution (training, impact assessments). Two months of focused effort (training completion, DPIA/FRIA finish) moves us to GREEN.

Regulatory exposure: Low to moderate. We have a documented governance system; no compliance breaches except the one policy incident (investigated and remediated). GDPR and EU AI Act supervisory authorities looking at staff training and impact assessments — both are underway.

Insurance / audit readiness: Good. Our governance registers and incident log provide evidence that we've thought about AI risks and taken deliberate steps.

Standards referenced

EU AI Act 2024/1689 (Article 4: AI literacy; Article 26: deployer obligations) · GDPR 2016/679 (Article 5: accountability; Article 35: impact assessment) · ISO/IEC 38507:2021 (board governance of information technology and AI).

i
How to use this template
  1. Quarterly update: Update this brief every quarter and present to the board or executive team alongside your governance scorecard.
  2. Use plain language: Replace jargon with plain English. Board members are not AI experts; help them understand the governance posture and risks in their own terms.
  3. Lead with decisions: Don't bury decisions needed in the middle. Make them explicit: "We need your approval to X by Y date."
  4. Cite standards: Brief the standards your organisation is governed by (EU AI Act, GDPR, ISO 42001, etc.). Board members understand "compliance" — frame governance as compliance.
  5. Track trends: Keep previous briefs to show progress: "Training completion up from 60% last quarter to 75% now."

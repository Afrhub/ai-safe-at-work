1. Reporting deadlines, start the clock now

Different regulators have different reporting windows. The table below is a quick reference. Note the trigger for each and the deadline from awareness, not from incident occurrence.

GDPR Art 33, controller → supervisory authority Personal-data breach likely to result in a risk: 72 hours from awareness.
GDPR Art 34, controller → data subjects Personal-data breach likely to result in a high risk: without undue delay.
EU AI Act Art 73, provider of high-risk AI Serious incident: 15 days (immediate if widespread or fatal: 2 days).
NIS2 Art 23, essential / important entity → CSIRT Significant incident: early warning 24 hours, notification 72 hours, final report 1 month.
DORA Art 19, financial entity → competent authority Major ICT-related incident: initial notification within hours per RTS.
Insurer notification Cyber / E&O policy clauses commonly require 72 hours from awareness.

10. Standards mapping

  • GDPR, Articles 33 (controller notification), 34 (data subject notification), 32 (security obligations).
  • EU AI Act, Article 73 (serious incident reporting by providers of high-risk AI systems).
  • NIS2, Article 23 (incident reporting for essential and important entities).
  • DORA, Article 19 (major ICT-related incident reporting) for in-scope financial entities.
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Annex A.6.2.6 (AI system operation: incident handling).
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Clause 6.1.3 (information security risk treatment); Annex A.5.24-A.5.28 (incident management); A.5.30 (ICT readiness for business continuity).
  • NIST AI RMF, Manage 4.1 (incident response and recovery).

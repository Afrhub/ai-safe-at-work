Governance inventory

AI Tool Register for [Company Name]

Track every AI tool in use, including consumer tools and shadow-AI instances. Record tool tier, data categories, approval status, and risk posture. Aligns with EU AI Act Art 4, Art 26 (deployer obligations), GDPR Art 5, ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 6.2.

Maintain this register in a shared spreadsheet. Review monthly. Update when tools change terms or when new tools are introduced.
Purpose and scope

Why we keep a tool register

An AI Tool Register records what tools your staff use, who has access, what data they handle, and what approval it has. It is:

  • Inventory, helps IT / security know what exists (especially "shadow AI" tools staff find and use on their own).
  • Governance, clarifies which tools are approved and which need review.
  • Risk, identifies data-handling risks and deployment posture.
  • Audit evidence, shows procurement oversight and AI governance maturity (EU AI Act Art 26, GDPR Art 32).

Scope

This register covers:

  1. All generative AI tools staff use for work (ChatGPT, Claude, Microsoft Copilot, Gemini, Perplexity, etc.).
  2. Both company-procured and personally-owned tools used for work purposes.
  3. Both consumer-tier (free / paid-personal) and enterprise-tier (business SLA, DPA).
  4. Both obvious usage (e.g. ChatGPT open in a tab) and embedded usage (e.g. AI features inside Slack, Salesforce, email).

Who maintains this register

Ownership: [DPO / IT lead / Security lead]

Input sources: IT asset scans, staff surveys (quarterly), incident reports, vendor change notifications, procurement requests.

Review cadence: Monthly. Update within one week of any significant tool change (terms of service change, data-handling shift, approval status change, new tool adoption).

Register columns explained

Tool name and vendor

Name the tool as staff call it (e.g. "ChatGPT", "Claude.ai", "Copilot in Microsoft 365", not just "ChatGPT", since that could mean the web app, the API, or the enterprise tenant, be specific). Include the tier if it matters for data handling: ChatGPT Enterprise ≠ ChatGPT Plus ≠ ChatGPT Free.

Approval status

Approved: Explicitly permitted by [approver, e.g. DPO, IT lead]. Staff may use it for any work within the AUP.

Pending: Not yet approved or rejected. Procurement is reviewing it (usually awaiting DPA review, vendor questionnaire response, or security assessment).

Blocked: Not permitted. Staff must not use it for any work. (Reason: terms incompatible with our data-handling policy, no DPA, vendor security posture unacceptable, etc.)

Data categories permitted

Which data types may be pasted into this tool, based on its tier and our DPA. Choose from:

  • Public-only: Only marketing content, published information, general knowledge.
  • Internal non-sensitive: General office management, non-confidential memos, published guidance.
  • Confidential business: Pricing, models, contracts (business data, not personal data).
  • Personal data (general): Names, emails, addresses, account numbers, subject to GDPR Article 5 (lawfulness, purpose, minimisation).
  • Special category (health, ethnicity, religion, etc.): High-sensitivity personal data. Rarely permitted; requires explicit legal basis and documented DPIA.

User count

How many staff have active access. Update quarterly. Helps spot shadow-AI sprawl (e.g. if your "personal Copilot" user count suddenly jumps 10×, investigate).

Data Protection Agreement (DPA) status

Yes: Signed Data Processing Agreement in place. File location: [path/to/DPA].

No: No DPA. Check whether the tool is used with personal data, if so, stop, escalate to DPO immediately.

Training provided

Have staff been trained on safe use of this tool? Yes / No / Ongoing. Even approved tools need usage guidance; point to your AUP or module assignment.

Risk posture / notes

One-line summary of the main governance concern, if any. Examples:

  • "Enterprise DPA in place, staff trained via Module 2."
  • "Consumer free tier; approval conditional on staff not entering personal data. Monitored via quarterly policy sweep."
  • "Terms of service prohibit customer data. Blocked for any customer-facing work; permitted for internal drafting only."
  • "Pending: awaiting vendor questionnaire response on sub-processors."

How to complete and maintain this register

  1. Start: Interview IT / procurement, survey staff (anonymous tool-use questionnaire), check email forwarding rules and SaaS integrations. List every tool.
  2. Baseline approval: For each tool, obtain a copy of the vendor's DPA (if it exists). Walk the tool and its DPA through your DPO. Record approval status, data categories permitted, training needs.
  3. Plan for shadow AI: Some tools will already be in use (staff found them personally). For each, decide: approve it (if safe) or request discontinuation. Record the decision.
  4. Maintain monthly: Spot-check for new tools (ask IT, check email aliases, scan Slack workspace, review procurement requests). When a tool vendor changes its terms (model update, data-handling change, sub-processor addition), the DPO re-reviews it within one week. Update the register same day.
  5. Communicate: Share the register with all managers once approved (by tool, status, and "what you can use for" summary). Link to your AUP.

Standards referenced

  • EU AI Act 2024/1689, Article 4 (AI literacy), Article 26 (deployer obligations on tool evaluation and ongoing monitoring).
  • GDPR 2016/679, Article 5 (lawfulness, fairness, transparency), Article 32 (security of processing including vendor due diligence).
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 6.2 (determining requirements for AI systems).
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Annex A.5.23 (information security of cloud services), A.6.3 (awareness training on tools and controls).

Rolling this out
  1. Set up the register in a shared spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Excel, Notion, etc.). Copy the columns above.
  2. Survey your IT team and managers: "What AI tools do you know of in use here?" Be specific about tier and account type.
  3. Walk the list through your DPO. For each tool, obtain the DPA and check your acceptable-data fit.
  4. Communicate the register to staff (by tool and approval status). Post the "approved tools" list prominently (intranet, handbook, next to the AUP).
  5. Assign a single owner (e.g. DPO, IT lead) to review and update monthly. Escalate any tool-term changes same week.

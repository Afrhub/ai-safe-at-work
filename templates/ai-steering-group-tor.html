The group that owns AI governance

AI Steering Group
Terms of Reference

Adopt this to stand up the group that owns AI in your organisation. It sets out why the group exists, who sits on it, what it decides, and how it reports. Fill the bracketed fields, take it to the group's first meeting, and approve it. Aligns with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 5 (leadership and roles).

Approve at the first meeting. Review annually.
1

Purpose

The AI Steering Group is the body accountable for the safe, lawful and effective use of artificial intelligence across [organisation]. It owns the AI governance framework, approves AI use cases, oversees risk, and reports to [the board / executive].

2

Scope

  • All AI and generative-AI tools used by staff, whether bought, built or embedded in other software.
  • All AI use cases that touch personal data, customers, employment decisions, or regulated activity.
  • The AI policies, registers and assessments listed in section 5.
  • Out of scope: day-to-day tool support (IT helpdesk) and individual HR matters.
3

Membership

Members may send a named deputy. The group may co-opt others (e.g. HR, a product lead) for specific items.

4

Responsibilities

  1. Own and maintain the AI governance framework and Acceptable Use Policy.
  2. Approve, reject or set conditions on AI use cases, using the AI Risk Assessment.
  3. Oversee the AI Use Case Register, Tool Register and Risk Register.
  4. Set and monitor AI awareness training; confirm staff completion.
  5. Review AI incidents and decide on regulator notification.
  6. Track EU AI Act and ISO/IEC 42001 obligations and confirm the organisation meets them.
  7. Report governance maturity and open risks to the board each quarter.
5

Documents the group owns

  • AI Acceptable Use Policy · AI Use Case Register · AI Tool Register
  • AI Risk Assessment (per use case) · AI Risk Register
  • AI Governance Roles Matrix · Training Register · Incident Form
6

Meetings, quorum and decisions

FrequencyQuarterly, plus on demand for urgent approvals
QuorumThe Chair (or deputy) plus at least [three] members, including data protection.
DecisionsBy consensus where possible; otherwise by majority, with the Chair holding the casting vote.
Urgent approvalsThe Chair and data protection lead may approve low-risk use cases between meetings and report them at the next.
MinutesDecisions and actions recorded and circulated within [five working days].
7

Reporting

The Chair reports to [the board / executive] each quarter on: use cases approved and rejected, open high risks, training completion, incidents, and progress against EU AI Act and ISO/IEC 42001 obligations.

8

Standards referenced

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 5 (leadership, roles, responsibilities and authorities).
  • EU AI Act, organisational accountability for AI risk and oversight.
i
Standing it up
  1. Fill the bracketed fields and the membership table with real names.
  2. Circulate before the first meeting so members read it in advance.
  3. Approve it as the first agenda item, then record the approval in section 8.
  4. Pair it with the AI Governance Roles Matrix so every decision has a clear owner.

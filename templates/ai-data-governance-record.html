Data governance

AI Data Governance Record for [Dataset Name]

One record per dataset feeding a high-risk or material AI system. Document where the data came from, its lawful basis, how representative it is, what bias and quality checks you ran, and how long you keep it. Aligns with EU AI Act Art 10 (data governance, relevance, representativeness, errors, completeness, bias), GDPR Art 5 & Art 9 (lawfulness, special-category data), ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 7.4 & Annex A data controls.

Keep one record per dataset. Review at each retraining and at least annually. Update when the source, lawful basis, or use changes.
Purpose and scope

Why we document data governance

An AI Data Governance Record captures the provenance, quality and bias posture of a single dataset that feeds an AI system. It is:

  • Provenance, shows where the data came from, on what lawful basis, and who is responsible for it.
  • Quality, records the accuracy, completeness and error checks you performed before the data was used.
  • Bias, documents how representative the data is of the target population and what under-represented groups or gaps remain.
  • Audit evidence, demonstrates that data governance and management practices are in place (EU AI Act Art 10, GDPR Art 5, ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 7.4).

Scope

Complete one record for each dataset that:

  1. Is used to train, validate or test a high-risk AI system (EU AI Act Annex III), or
  2. Materially affects the behaviour of any AI system your organisation relies on for a significant decision, or
  3. Contains personal data or special-category data processed by an AI system.

Where a system draws on several datasets, keep a separate record for each and link them all to the same AI System Card.

Who maintains this record

Ownership: [Dataset owner / Data steward]

Input sources: data acquisition and procurement records, DPIA, vendor data sheets, labelling / annotation logs, bias and quality test outputs.

Review cadence: At every retraining and at least annually. Update within one week of any change to the data source, lawful basis, or intended use.

Field notes

Source & provenance

State whether the data is first-party (you collected it), licensed (bought or licensed from a supplier), publicly scraped, or synthetic. For licensed or scraped data, record the licence terms and whether they permit AI training. Scraped data carries the highest lawful-basis and copyright risk, be explicit.

Lawful basis & special-category handling

Record the GDPR Article 6 basis for processing the personal data in this dataset. If it contains special-category data (health, ethnicity, religion, biometrics, sexual orientation, etc.), you also need an Article 9 condition and, in almost all cases, a documented DPIA. If neither is in place, stop and escalate to the DPO.

Representativeness and bias

EU AI Act Article 10 requires training, validation and testing data to be relevant, sufficiently representative, and free of errors as far as possible. Describe the target population, compare it to what the dataset actually contains, and name any groups that are thin or missing. Record the bias test you ran (method, protected attributes, metric, result and date), not just that one was "done".

Data-quality and labelling

Note the accuracy, completeness and error checks performed, and how labels or annotations were produced and quality-assured. Article 10 expects appropriate data-preparation processing, including annotation, labelling, cleaning and the examination for possible biases.

Worked example

Field Example entry
Dataset name & version Loan-eligibility-training-set v2.1
Owner J. Okoro, Data Steward, Credit Risk
Linked AI system(s) Automated loan-eligibility scorer (high-risk, Annex III)
Source & provenance First-party: internal loan applications 2021-2025
Lawful basis / Art 9 Contract (Art 6(1)(b)). No special-category data (ethnicity excluded at source)
Representativeness Serves UK adult applicants; under-25s and rural postcodes under-represented (<4%)
Bias checks + result Demographic-parity + equal-opportunity by sex and age band, June 2026. Age-band gap 0.09, above 0.05 threshold, reweighting applied, re-tested
Retention 6 years from last decision, then deleted (aligned to lending records policy)
Review date Reviewed 2026-06-30; next due at retraining or 2027-06-30

Standards referenced

  • EU AI Act 2024/1689, Article 10 (data and data governance, relevance, representativeness, freedom from errors, completeness, and examination for bias in training, validation and testing data).
  • GDPR 2016/679, Article 5 (lawfulness, fairness, purpose limitation, minimisation, accuracy), Article 9 (processing of special categories of personal data).
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 7.4 (documented information) and Annex A controls on data for AI systems (data provenance, quality and preparation).
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Annex A.5.34 (privacy and protection of PII), A.8.10 (information deletion).
i
Rolling this out
  1. List every dataset that feeds a high-risk or material AI system. Create one record per dataset.
  2. For each, gather the acquisition record, licence or consent evidence, and any DPIA. Confirm the lawful basis with your DPO.
  3. Run and record representativeness, bias and data-quality checks before the data is used to train or evaluate a model.
  4. Link each record to its AI System Card so provenance is traceable from the system down to the data.
  5. Assign a single owner to review each record at every retraining and at least annually. Escalate any source or lawful-basis change the same week.

What good looks like, and by when

AI Objectives Register

Governance only improves if you set targets and track them. Record each AI objective, how you will measure it, who owns it, and when it is due. Review at every Steering Group meeting. Aligns with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 6.2.

Each objective should be specific and measurable. Review quarterly.

How to set a good objective

  • Measurable: a number or a yes/no, not "improve awareness".
  • Owned: one named person, not a committee.
  • Time-bound: a quarter or a date, so it can be late or done.
  • Linked: ties back to a real risk in your Risk Register.

Standards referenced

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Clause 6.2 (AI objectives and planning to achieve them).

Keeping it live
  1. Set three to six objectives — enough to matter, few enough to track.
  2. Put status updates on every Steering Group agenda.
  3. Close objectives that are done; carry over or revise the rest.

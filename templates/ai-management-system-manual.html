AI Management System Manual

This is the cover document for your whole AI governance system. It says what the system covers, who runs it, and which records prove it works, then points to every other template. Adopt this first, fill the bracketed fields, and keep it under version control. Structured to ISO/IEC 42001 clauses 4 to 10.

Clause 4

Context and scope

This AI Management System (AIMS) covers [the AI tools, use cases and teams in scope]. It exists to make sure AI is used safely, lawfully and effectively across [organisation].

  • Internal context: the teams using AI, the systems it touches, the data involved.
  • External context: the EU AI Act, GDPR, sector regulators, customer expectations.
  • Interested parties: staff, customers, regulators, vendors, the board.
  • Out of scope: [anything explicitly excluded].
Clause 5

Leadership and policy

Top management owns the AIMS through the AI Steering Group. The AI Policy and Acceptable Use Policy set the rules. Roles and decision rights are defined in the AI Governance Roles Matrix.

  • Governing body: AI Steering Group (see its Terms of Reference).
  • Policies: AI Acceptable Use Policy and supporting standards.
  • Roles and authorities: AI Governance Roles Matrix.
Clause 6

Planning

  • Risks and opportunities: assessed per use case in the AI Risk Assessment and tracked in the AI Risk Register.
  • Objectives: set and tracked in the AI Objectives Register.
  • Impact: data and rights impacts assessed via the DPIA and, where relevant, the FRIA.
Clause 7

Support

  • Competence and awareness: the AI Safe@Work course; completion held in the Training Register; reinforced by the AI Awareness Poster.
  • Resources: [budget, tooling, people assigned to the AIMS].
  • Documented information: this manual and every template, kept under version control.
Clause 8

Operation

  • New AI tools and use cases pass through the AI Risk Assessment before approval.
  • Approved tools and use cases are recorded in the AI Tool Register and AI Use Case Register.
  • Vendors are checked with the AI Vendor Due Diligence Assessment.
  • Problems are handled through the Incident Form and the incident process.
Clause 9

Performance evaluation

  • Monitoring: the AI Monitoring & KPI Dashboard tracks the measures that matter.
  • Internal audit: run on a schedule using the AI Internal Audit Template.
  • Management review: the Steering Group reviews the AIMS each quarter and reports to the board.
Clause 10

Improvement

Findings from audits, incidents and reviews feed corrective actions. Actions are tracked to closure and the relevant templates are updated. The aim is a system that gets stronger each cycle.

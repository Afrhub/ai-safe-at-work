Risk assessment and tracking

AI Risk Register for [Company Name]

Identify, assess, and track AI-specific risks: data leakage, hallucination, bias, vendor lock-in, and compliance gaps. Record severity, mitigation actions, and responsible owners. Aligns with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 8.2, EU AI Act Article 27, GDPR Articles 32, 35.

Maintain in a shared spreadsheet. Review quarterly or when a new AI use case is introduced. Escalate High risks immediately.
Purpose and scope

Why we keep a risk register

An AI Risk Register records the risks we've identified in our AI use, what could go wrong, how likely it is, how severe it would be, and what we're doing about it. It:

  • Makes risk visible, forces a structured conversation about what we're actually exposed to.
  • Assigns ownership, ensures someone is responsible for each risk (not just a general concern).
  • Drives mitigation, converts risk awareness into concrete actions (training, controls, process changes).
  • Provides audit evidence, shows regulators and insurers that we've thought about AI risks and taken deliberate steps (ISO 42001 Clause 8.2, GDPR Article 32).

Scope

This register covers risks arising from:

  1. Data risks: Personal data leakage, confidential-data exposure, unintended data retention by vendors.
  2. Output risks: Hallucinations, inaccuracy, bias, misinformation being relied upon for decisions.
  3. Vendor risks: Vendor closure, term-of-service changes, data-handling practices, sub-processor changes, model updates.
  4. Operational risks: Shadow AI, unapproved tools, overreliance without human review, skill erosion.
  5. Compliance risks: GDPR breaches, EU AI Act violations, regulatory gaps, audit failures.
  6. Reputational risks: AI-generated content errors, bias allegations, misuse of AI tools.

Risk assessment framework

Likelihood

High: This risk has already occurred or is very likely to occur within 12 months (e.g. staff are already pasting personal data into free ChatGPT).

Medium: This risk could plausibly occur within 12–24 months under normal circumstances (e.g. a vendor changes its DPA terms without notice).

Low: This risk is unlikely within 2 years or would require several preconditions to occur (e.g. a vendor is bought out and acquires worse data practices).

Impact

High: If this risk occurs, it causes serious harm: GDPR fine or breach notification required, regulatory investigation, significant reputational damage, customer contracts at risk, or business continuity disrupted.

Medium: Significant but manageable impact: operational disruption, internal policy breach, customer complaint, remediation cost >£10,000, or single-customer relationship damage.

Low: Minor impact: localised inconvenience, internal process fix, minor cost (<£10,000), or no external visibility.

Register columns explained

Risk category

Choose from: Data leakage · Hallucination · Bias · Vendor risk · Compliance gap · Shadow AI · Operational.

Description

Specific risk statement. Example: "Customer personal data (names, emails) pasted into free ChatGPT by support team." Not: "Data risk." Be concrete.

Current controls

What are we already doing to reduce this risk? Example: "AUP training, approval list excludes free tools, monthly AI-use survey." If no controls, write "None."

Likelihood & impact

Use the framework above. Likelihood × Impact = Overall rating.

Owner

A named person responsible for managing this risk. Not a title, a name. They lead mitigation, monitor trends, report status monthly.

Mitigation action

Specific action to reduce the risk. Examples:

  • "Deploy ChatGPT Enterprise as approved tool; retire free-tier usage by [date]."
  • "Implement vendor questionnaire review process; assess [Tool X] by [date]."
  • "Quarterly bias audit of AI-assisted hiring decisions."
  • "Monthly survey: staff to report shadow-AI tools and blockers; escalate to IT."

Mitigation due date

When should this mitigation action be complete? For High risks, within 30 days. For Medium, within 90 days. For Low, within 6 months.

Status

Open: Action not yet started or in progress.

In progress: Work underway, on track to meet due date.

Complete: Mitigation action finished; residual risk re-assessed.

Deferred: Deferral approved by [role]; new target date recorded.

How to establish and maintain the register

  1. Initial workshop: Convene IT, DPO, security, operations, and a line manager. Brainstorm: "What are our AI risks?" Use the risk categories above and Module 4–6 incident examples. Record 8–15 risks.
  2. Assess each: For each risk, estimate likelihood and impact. Plot on the matrix. Assign an owner (named person, not role).
  3. Prioritise: High and Critical risks get mitigation plans within 30 days. Medium risks get plans within 90 days. Low risks are monitored.
  4. Own and execute: Each risk owner leads their mitigation action, reports monthly. Escalate blockers to [DPO / CISO / leadership].
  5. Review quarterly: Have existing risks improved? Are new risks emerging (new tools, vendor changes)? Update the register same week. If a High risk is resolved, move it to archive (keep for audit history).

Standards referenced

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Clause 8.2 (risk and opportunity identification), Clause 8.3 (compliance obligations), Clause 9.1 (monitoring and measurement).
  • EU AI Act 2024/1689, Article 27 (deployer obligations to assess and mitigate risks).
  • GDPR 2016/679, Article 32 (security of processing, including risk assessment), Article 35 (Data Protection Impact Assessment).

Getting started
  1. Set up a shared spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Notion, etc.) with columns: Risk ID, Category, Description, Likelihood, Impact, Rating, Owner, Mitigation action, Due date, Status, Last updated.
  2. Run a 90-minute workshop with IT, DPO, and operations. Use Modules 3–6 and the eight risk categories above to prompt discussion.
  3. Assess each risk using the likelihood/impact framework. Plot on the matrix to get overall rating.
  4. For every High/Critical risk, define a specific mitigation action with a due date and owner name.
  5. Communicate to leadership. Escalate High-risk mitigations for approval before executing.
  6. Review and update monthly. Assign a single owner to shepherd the register and chase progress.

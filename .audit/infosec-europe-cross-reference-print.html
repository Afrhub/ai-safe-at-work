4 · The defensible niche

One-line wedge The Article 4 AI-literacy pack KnowBe4 doesn't ship and OneTrust doesn't sell — built for SMB staff, priced for MSP resale, evidenced for the EU auditor.

Three reasons it holds against the 20 direct competitors at Infosec Europe:

  1. Regulatory anchor with a date. EU AI Act Article 4 (literacy obligation) entered force 2 February 2025. Every deployer — SMB included — must demonstrate it. No exhibitor leads with this. We do.
  2. Audience nobody serves end-to-end. SAT vendors serve generalist staff (phishing-led). AI-Gov vendors serve enterprise legal teams. Model-security vendors serve MLOps. SMB end-user · learning AI specifically · with EU framing — nobody owns it.
  3. Price + channel shape no incumbent can match without cannibalising. KnowBe4 cannot drop to £499/mo wholesale without nuking enterprise margin. OneTrust cannot strip down to staff literacy without diluting platform narrative. Bottom-of-market AI-literacy slot is structurally open.

# Role-track outline — AI Governance Officer

> Scope-locking outline. Full content authored at Phase 2+ build slot.

## Audience

The person formally named as the AI Governance Officer (AIGO), Head of AI, AI Compliance Lead, or equivalent inside an organisation. Often a new role created in response to the EU AI Act. Sits between the DPO, the CISO, and the COO. May report to the Board's risk committee.

Distinct from the DPO track in `module-dpo.html` (v1) and from the Governance & Procurement track above. The AIGO does both functions plus AI strategy and AI risk reporting upward. They are accountable for the AI Management System under ISO/IEC 42001 if the organisation adopts it.

## Why a dedicated track

Per `DOCTRINE.md § Audience hierarchy` (P1) and `§ Strategic doctrine + growth framework → Role-based learning expansion`, this role is rising in volume across EU mid-market driven by Article 4 + Article 26 obligations. The persona buys deep governance content and often holds the budget.

A dedicated track also serves the cohort that wants to demonstrate **AI Management System** competence under ISO/IEC 42001 — content can later be partnered with an accredited body (PECB, BSI) into an AIGO certificate.

## Standards in scope

Whole-of-doctrine — EU AI Act in full, GDPR in full, ISO/IEC 42001 in full, ISO/IEC 27001 in full, plus NIST AI RMF · ISO/IEC 38507 · ISO/IEC 23894 · ENISA Multilayer Framework for AI Cybersecurity · CSA AICM · sector overlays as relevant.

8. Vendor and sub-processor management for AI

10. AI incident response and serious-incident reporting (EU AI Act Article 73 + GDPR Article 33–34)
11. Board reporting on AI — the quarterly pack
12. The AI Management System internal audit programme
13. Continuous improvement — turning findings into the next cycle's actions
14. Final assessment

# Role-track outline — Governance &amp; Procurement

> Scope-locking outline. Full content authored at Phase 2+ build slot.

## Audience

The procurement lead, vendor manager, or commercial governance owner inside an SMB or mid-market organisation. Often DPO-adjacent but distinct: the persona who decides whether to **buy** the AI tool, not whether to use it. Their decision shapes downstream operational risk for years.

## Why a dedicated track

The universal course covers end-user decisions about AI usage. This track covers organisational decisions about AI acquisition. The two sit upstream and downstream of each other; both matter to procurement-readiness gates 6 (role-based tracks) and 7 (templates pack).

The procurement persona is also the **first paying customer of `vendor-questionnaire.html`**. This track gives them the language and the framework to actually use that questionnaire as a buying tool, not just a compliance form.

## Standards in scope

EU AI Act Articles 13, 25, 26, 27 (provider + deployer interaction) · GDPR Articles 28, 30, 44–50 · ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 8.3 + Annex A.5, A.10 · ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A.5.10, A.5.23, A.5.34 · CSA AICM · OWASP LLM Top 10 (procurement view).

## Draft module list (8–10 modules)

1. Why AI procurement is different — the supplier-due-diligence questions traditional templates miss
2. The 37-question vendor questionnaire — how to use it, how to score it, how to follow up
3. DPA review for AI vendors — the four clauses that matter most
4. Sub-processor disclosure obligations — what to demand, how to verify
5. EU AI Act Article 13 — provider obligations you can lean on at buying time
6. EU AI Act Article 26 — deployer obligations you take on when you buy
7. Sector procurement frameworks (DORA Art 13, NYDFS Part 500, NHS DCB0129, etc.) and how AI vendors map to them
8. Contracting for change — model swap clauses, term-change notification, exit-data clauses
9. The buy-vs-build-vs-defer decision tree for AI
10. Final assessment

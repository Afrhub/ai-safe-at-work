# Sector overlay outline — Public Sector

> Scope-locking outline. Full content authored at Phase 2+ build slot.

## Target audience

UK + EU public-sector small bodies: local councils, NDPBs, ALBs, multi-academy trusts, FE colleges, smaller HEIs, NHS-trust admin, regional fire services, regional police support staff, charity-sector partners delivering public services under commissioning. Plus the MSPs serving them under government frameworks (e.g. G-Cloud).

## Why a sector overlay

Public-sector AI use is uniquely regulated:

1. **EU AI Act Annex III** lists a long set of public-services AI use cases as high-risk (education admission, welfare allocation, law enforcement, migration).
2. **UK AI Playbook** (Cabinet Office / GDS / DSIT) lays out government-specific AI usage expectations.
3. **FRIA** (Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment, EU AI Act Article 27) is **mandatory** for public-sector deployers in many cases.
4. **Procurement** is dramatically different — government-framework rules, transparency obligations, public accountability when an AI decision affects a citizen.

## Standards layered on top of universal course

- EU AI Act Annex III + Article 27 (FRIA) + Article 26 (deployer)
- UK AI Playbook (Cabinet Office / GDS)
- UK Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS)
- OMB M-24-10 / M-25-21 (US, cross-reference)
- FedRAMP (US, cross-reference for cloud)
- US Executive Orders on AI use in government (cross-reference)
- ICO guidance on AI in public services
- CNIL guidance on AI in public sector (France)
- BNetzA + BfDI guidance (Germany)
- ENISA Multilayer Framework for AI Cybersecurity (where relevant)

## Draft module list (overlay)

1. The public-sector governance layer — Annex III + UK AI Playbook + national-regulator overlays
2. The public-sector never-paste list — citizen identifiers, casework records, welfare-claim files, education records
3. FRIA in practice — when it is required, how to scope it, what good looks like
4. UK Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard — publishing an ATRS record
5. AI-assisted decisions affecting citizens — Article 22 GDPR + EU AI Act + judicial-review risk
6. Procurement under government frameworks — G-Cloud, Crown Commercial Service, EU public procurement, AI-specific clauses
7. Public accountability and transparency reporting on AI usage
8. Incident escalation — which regulator hears about an AI incident in a public-sector context
9. Final assessment

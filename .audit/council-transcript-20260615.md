# LLM Council — Relevance vs the AI-governance certification landscape
**Date:** 2026-06-15

## Framed question
Is AI Safe@Work (free EU AI-literacy course for non-technical SMB staff; paid layer = certificates, SCORM, MSP white-label, governance consultancy) still relevant given paid individual certs (IAPP AIGP ~$1.1–1.8k, IEEE CertifAIEd, Tonex, Georgetown, Gen-AI risk exams)? Where is the real gap/exposure — and should it stay the free workforce-literacy layer beneath AIGP-style credentials, or change?

## Advisor responses (de-anonymised)
- **Contrarian (Response C):** Certs aren't your competition — that's the problem. Free core trains zero-willingness-to-pay staff; the paid buyer (owner/MSP) isn't engaged. Paid features (certs/SCORM/scoring) are commodity LMS table-stakes; standards-map + templates are trivially copied. Art 4 has no cert requirement, no SMB penalty with teeth yet — "aspirin for a headache buyers don't feel." Pick one cheque-signer: MSP tool or consultancy. Free course is a lead magnet, not a north star.
- **First Principles (Response A):** "Compete with AIGP" is the wrong frame. AIGP credentials one individual; your job is letting an SMB discharge a legal obligation across the whole workforce, provably. Buyer = employer/MSP/insurer, not the learner. The Article 4 attestation IS the product and you're giving it away. The course is COGS; the receipt is the product. Sit orthogonal to AIGP, not beneath.
- **Expansionist (Response D):** You're upstream of a 100×-larger pool (50–200 non-credentialled staff/company). Free + no-login + role-tracked = the default deployment substrate; every learner is a data point → the only real map of where EU SMB AI-competence breaks, which prices insurance and underwrites consultancy. Adjacencies: insurer verification layer; MSP white-label engine (sell distribution, not seats); become the published standard. Monetise the proof.
- **Outsider (Response B):** These aren't comparable — cafe hygiene leaflet vs Michelin auditor cert. "$2–5k/employee" isn't the receptionist. I don't even know what "AI safety" means for my staff, or who audits it. Gap: nobody teaches the ordinary employee plainly/cheaply/at scale; certs structurally can't. Real risk: SMBs don't yet believe they need this.
- **Executor (Response E):** Market's validated; you built a product + price book before one human paid — the gap is distribution. Monday: (1) sell the £4,995 consultancy first (one scared buyer, a phone call), email 10 EU SMBs a free 30-min Art 4 readiness check; (2) MSP white-label is a partnership — first MSP 90 days free for case study + logo; (3) put a £29 certificate button on the existing flow this week.

## Peer review highlights
- **Strongest:** 4/5 reviewers → A (First Principles): "course is COGS, the receipt is the product" named the load-bearing insight; D second.
- **Biggest blind spot:** A and D assume the attestation already has market value — but Art 4 has no certification requirement / no SMB penalty with teeth yet (C's point). E asserts "market validated" without testing demand.
- **All five missed:** (1) the **trigger event** — compliance sells on a forcing function (first enforcement, insurer questionnaire, customer-contract clause, audit demand), not on a law existing; (2) **incumbent absorption** — once the attestation has value, every LMS/MSP/HR-compliance vendor + insurers bundle a free literacy module → category becomes a feature, not a company; (3) **localization** — "EU" = 24 languages + member-state enforcement; (4) **liability** of issuing the receipt; (5) **content staleness** — the clause-map decays and needs upkeep.

LLM Council · Verdict

Still relevant, but you're defending the wrong layer.

Is AI Safe@Work still relevant against IAPP AIGP & the paid-cert landscape, and where is the real gap? Should it stay the free workforce-literacy layer beneath AIGP-style credentials, or change?

Where the council agrees (high confidence)

  • The certs are not your competitors. AIGP/IEEE/Tonex credential one individual for a CV. You discharge a whole workforce's obligation. Different buyer, different job, you're orthogonal to AIGP, not beneath it.
  • The learner never pays. The cheque-signer is the employer, MSP, or insurer carrying the risk, and you're not yet engaging them.
  • The real product is the proof, not the course. The Article 4 attestation + audit pack is the valuable asset; the course/SCORM/scoring are commodity table-stakes. You're giving away the receipt and charging for the COGS.
  • MSP white-label is the engine, sell distribution, not seats. One MSP = a whole client base.
  • The gap is not relevance, it's demand activation + distribution. Zero paying customers, and SMBs may not yet feel the pain.

Where the council clashes

Does the compliance "receipt" have value yet?

Build the proof-layer now
First Principles · Expansionist

The attestation IS the product; own the evidence layer + the data map before anyone else. Monetise proof.

No forcing function = no sale
Contrarian (Executor splits)

Art 4 has no cert requirement, no SMB penalty with teeth yet, "aspirin for a headache buyers don't feel." Validate cheaply before betting the build.

Reasonable advisors split because the strategy is right but its timing depends on enforcement that hasn't fully arrived. The flywheel is real; the question is whether it spins in 2026 or 2028.

Blind spots the peer review caught

  • The trigger event. Compliance products sell on a forcing function, first enforcement action, an insurer questionnaire, a customer-contract clause, an audit demand, not on a law merely existing. Yours isn't named yet.
  • Incumbent absorption. The moment the attestation has value, every LMS / MSP-security-awareness vendor (KnowBe4) / HR-compliance tool / insurer bolts on a free literacy module → the category becomes a feature, not a company. Win a forcing-function + distribution lock first.
  • Localization. "EU" = 24 languages + member-state enforcement. "Plain English" doesn't cover the workforce, a real moat and a real cost.
  • Liability of the receipt. If you sell the proof, you may inherit risk when a "certified" employee causes an incident. Unpriced.
  • Content staleness. The clause-map decays as guidance evolves; an outdated map is a liability, not an asset. Maintenance is ongoing cost.

The recommendation

Yes, still relevant, but reposition. Stop framing against AIGP and stop calling free literacy the north star. Reposition the paid product as the EU AI Act Article 4 compliance-evidence layer (attestation + audit pack + training register), sold to the employer / MSP / insurer, with the free course as the lead-magnet/COGS.

Go to market through MSPs now and insurers next, distribution, not direct seats. But because the receipt's value rides on a forcing function that isn't fully here, don't bet the company on the flywheel yet: run cheap demand probes this week to find who already feels the pain, while quietly building the evidence-pack + data moat that incumbents can't copy fast.

The one thing to do first

Phone 5 EU MSPs and 5 SMBs with a single offer, "free 30-minute EU AI Act Article 4 readiness check." It validates demand, surfaces the real forcing function, and sources your first white-label/consultancy deal in one move. (Pair it with a £29 "verified certificate" button on the live course as a zero-cost willingness-to-pay probe.)

Advisor responses

The Contrarian, "name who signs the cheque"

Certs aren't your competition, that's the problem. Free core trains zero-willingness-to-pay staff; the paid buyer (owner/MSP) isn't engaged. Certs/SCORM/scoring are commodity LMS table-stakes; standards-map + templates are trivially copied. Art 4 has no cert requirement, no SMB penalty with teeth yet, aspirin for a headache buyers don't feel. Pick one cheque-signer: MSP tool or consultancy. The free course is a lead magnet, not a north star.

The First Principles Thinker, "the course is COGS, the receipt is the product" ★ strongest (4/5)

"Compete with AIGP" is the wrong frame. AIGP credentials one individual; your job is letting an SMB discharge a legal obligation across the whole workforce, provably. Buyer = employer/MSP/insurer, not the learner. The Article 4 attestation IS your product and you're giving it away. Lead with proof-of-compliance, not learning. Sit orthogonal to AIGP, it certifies the one expert, you certify the other 40 people that expert is responsible for.

The Expansionist, "monetise the proof, own the data map"

You're upstream of a 100×-larger pool: 50–200 non-credentialled staff per company who create the liability. Free + no-login + role-tracked = the default deployment substrate; every learner is a data point → the only real map of where EU SMB AI-competence breaks, which prices insurance and underwrites consultancy. Adjacencies: insurer verification layer; MSP white-label engine (sell distribution, not seats); become the published standard. Stay free at core, monetise the proof.

The Outsider, "SMBs don't yet believe they need this"

These aren't comparable, like a cafe hygiene leaflet vs a Michelin auditor cert. "$2–5k/employee" isn't the receptionist. I don't know what "AI safety" means for my staff, or who audits it, that fear is your real product and I can't tell if it's real. Gap: nobody teaches the ordinary employee plainly, cheaply, at scale; the certs structurally can't. Your risk isn't AIGP, it's that small businesses don't yet believe they need you.

The Executor, "nobody's been asked to pay yet; ask Monday"

Market's validated; the problem is you built a product and a price book before one human paid you. The gap is distribution. Stop touching the product.

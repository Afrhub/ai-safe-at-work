# LLM Council transcript — Attest AI monetisation & pricing
2026-07-19 · NOT COMMITTED (public repo; commercially sensitive, same treatment as subscription-model.pdf)

## Question (framed)
How should Attest AI be monetised, and how much should it charge? Structure + numbers, with reasoning.
Context given to all advisors: product set (Safe@Work course, AIMP platform, Plus Pack, consultancy), current live pricing (Tier 1 £1,000 one-off incl. all templates; Tier 2 unpriced), locked MSP doctrine (£499/£1,499/£3–5k/mo + 70/30), RORtech founding promise, market stats (1.4M UK employer businesses, 12,867 MSPs), ChatGPT proposal (£99/user/yr; £12.99/user/mo), stage (solo founder, zero customers, invoice-only).

Anonymisation map for peer review: A = Executor · B = Contrarian · C = Outsider · D = Expansionist · E = First Principles.

## Advisor responses

### The Contrarian (B)
The fatal flaw is that every option on the table prices a product nobody has yet paid for, while the channel doctrine was "locked" before a single customer existed. That's backwards. You've committed to MSP tier prices, revenue splits, and a 50%-off founding promise to RORtech, all downstream of end-customer pricing you haven't set. Lock the retail price first or the channel maths is fiction. (1) £12.99/user/month including training is arithmetic self-harm: a 10-person SME pays £1,559/yr, but Tier 1 sells training PLUS all 27 templates for £1,000 one-off, forever. The one-off cannibalises the subscription; kill the £1,000 tier or make it a time-boxed pilot. (2) Per-user platform pricing on an org-level product means the buyer's rational move is 2 seats; price the platform per-company in bands (£149/£299/£599/mo by headcount), per-user only for training. (3) The MSP margin has never been reconciled: 70/30 on £99/user training is ~£70/user/yr to the MSP while they pay you £499–£5k/mo. At what volume does an MSP break even? RORtech will do that sum and the promise collapses. (4) The real risk isn't the number, it's invoice-only friction. Validate with 3 direct sales at per-company pricing before honouring any MSP promise.

### The First Principles Thinker (E)
With zero customers the question is "what price gets ten strangers to pay quickly enough to prove the problem exists?" The job being bought is fear discharge: "the EU AI Act says I must do something; make me defensibly compliant with minimal effort", bought by an owner/ops director, once, at organisation level. The unit of value is the organisation, not the seat; per-user platform pricing taxes exactly the behaviour you want (rolling training out to everyone) and breaks MSP margins. Seats are tier boundaries only (≤25 / ≤100 / 100+). Recommendation: one SKU, Attest AI Complete, £199/mo or £1,990/yr (≤25 staff; £399/mo ≤100): training + platform + templates. The £1,000 one-off is your worst enemy; retire it. MSP channel only works at the £399+ tier or with volume; check RORtech's client book before honouring the founding promise publicly. Consultancy is the real first-revenue engine: sell three consulting projects and GIVE them the platform; their usage is validation, their logos are the pricing page. Anchor at CyberSmart, not KnowBe4: you're compliance insurance, not training content.

### The Expansionist (D)
The money is in the channel maths: 12,867 MSPs × 20 SME clients each is a quarter-million businesses reachable without direct sales. Attest can be the white-label AI-governance layer every UK MSP suddenly needs, the way MSPs bolted on cyber-essentials packages in 2018. Structure: Safe@Work £15/user/yr (£499/yr minimum, KnowBe4 parity — £99/user is fantasy at zero customers); Platform flat org bands £149/£299/£599 per month (training included) making MSP margin arithmetic trivial (~£100–420/mo per client at 70/30); keep the £1,000 bundle as a front-door tripwire renamed "Foundation Pack"; reframe the £3–5k MSP tier as "Compliance Practice in a Box". Bigger upside: Article 4 is EU-wide, FR/DE already shelled; an attestation badge/registry (Attest as the attestation authority) at £499/yr for the mark alone, later. Ship flat bands now, sign RORtech at 50% off, let 30 MSPs become your salesforce.

### The Outsider (C)
As a first-time visitor: you're asking £1,000 by invoice before I can see almost anything; nav items redirect to a checkout — reads broken or scammy. The founder card literally says "Founder name / Short bio goes here"; I'd close the tab. No named human, no address, no customers, no card payment: I'm not raising a £1,000 PO. Tier 2 says "Monthly subscription" with no number; a blank price means "expensive" or "unfinished" and I leave rather than email. Per-seat is the only framing an office manager understands. The ChatGPT numbers are in the believable zone (£99/user/yr training computes instantly against security-awareness training; £12.99/user/mo ≈ £4,700/yr for a 30-person office, signable without a board conversation). Before I'd pay anything: card checkout, a free sample module or clickable demo, a real founder bio, a UK address, and un-paywall the glossary and standards map — charging for a glossary is the single strangest thing on the site. Publish the numbers; blank pricing costs more than any wrong number.

### The Executor (A)
Monday morning: publish three numbers, keep invoicing manual, sell to RORtech by Friday. Kill per-user pricing (no billing infra; flat company bands match manual Supabase provisioning). Publish today: Safe@Work + Plus Pack stays £1,000 one-off (≤25 staff; £1,750 for 26–100); Attest AI Platform £249/mo (≤25) / £499/mo (≤100) billed annually by invoice (£2,490/£4,990, training included); consultancy unchanged. RORtech this week: founding slot at the £499 tier, 50% off year 1, 70/30 on awareness subs, quarterly reconciliation on a shared sheet; ask for a signed one-page order and a first client candidate by month end. Do not build billing; manual invoicing caps ~30 customers and you have zero — that's headroom. The test: one direct £1,000 order and one RORtech signature in 30 days; if not, the problem was never pricing.

## Peer reviews (all five, verbatim findings)
- Review 1: Strongest B (cannibalisation is the decision-relevant finding). Blind spot D (TAM fantasy; ignores solo capacity, invoice ops). All missed: delivery cost/founder capacity as the pricing constraint; professional-indemnity liability of selling "defensible compliance" (terms, insurance, what happens when a customer relying on templates gets fined).
- Review 2: Strongest B. Blind spot D (premature scaling; keeps the cannibalising £1,000 tripwire; hand-waves capacity). All missed: IP licensing/white-label legality and liability terms shape channel pricing more than the split; VAT mechanics on rev share; nobody proposed asking RORtech what its clients actually pay for comparable lines — one call replaces the debate.
- Review 3: Strongest B (per-company logic; demands MSP break-even before RORtech). Blind spot D (fantasy arithmetic; attestation-authority liability). All missed: consultancy as the pricing instrument — charge £3–5k engagements now and use them to discover willingness-to-pay before publishing platform numbers; competitor prices asserted, never verified.
- Review 4: Strongest B; E close second ("sell consultancy, give the platform"). Blind spot D (tenth step before step one; self-contradiction on the £1,000 bundle). All missed: founder capacity caps how many £199–499/mo accounts one person can serve; VAT-registration threshold effects near £90k; RORtech's clients as the only obtainable real data point this week.
- Review 5: Strongest B. Blind spot D; also C's per-seat advice conflicts with org-level economics. All missed: EU AI Act enforcement timing as urgency-based packaging; PI insurance before revenue ("one bad audit ends the business"); reframe RORtech as design partner rather than first sale; benchmark evidence for every asserted price.

# LLM Council transcript — Band pricing, grid visibility, transparency
2026-07-19 (session 2) · NOT COMMITTED (public repo; commercially sensitive)

## Question (framed)
Should Attest AI price by headcount RANGES (1–20 / 21–50 / 51–100 / 101–150…)? Should both
Tier 1 (Foundation) and Tier 2 (Platform) be shown at every band (full public grid)? Is showing
all prices at all times worth it — pros and cons? MSP/partner pricing stays hidden, partner-only
like rebates — does that work?
Context: prior council verdict (Foundation £990/yr ≤25; Platform £249/£499 per month in two org
bands; publish numbers; reconcile RORtech before honouring channel tiers). Solo founder, zero
customers, manual invoicing.

Anonymisation map: A = Outsider · B = Executor · C = First Principles · D = Contrarian · E = Expansionist.

## Advisor responses (verbatim)

### The Contrarian (D) — peer-ranked strongest 3/5
A four-plus band public grid for two tiers is a spreadsheet, not a pricing page. With zero customers every band beyond the first two is fiction — no willingness-to-pay data at 51–100 or 101–150; publishing invented numbers locks you in; your first 120-person prospect screenshots the grid and negotiates against it forever. Fine bands invite reverse gaming (a 22-person firm "counts" 20) with no verification mechanism, and the grid hands procurement your margin model. Transparency is being asked backwards: publish ENTRY prices (a blank price loses visitors) but full transparency at the top end bites — a 150-person org might pay £15k/yr; a public £499/mo caps them at £6k and you never find out. Two published bands, "talk to us" above 100. Hidden MSP pricing is fine in principle but fails HERE: the RORtech tiers were never reconciled with the direct prices; if an MSP can undercut your public page — or resell above it while the client sees your direct price — someone looks dishonest. Reconcile first; add a clause: MSP resale price ≥ your public direct price.

### The Executor (B) — peer-ranked strongest 2/5
You can operate exactly as many bands as you can invoice by hand — four is the ceiling; yesterday's two-band structure was fine. Fine bands multiply boundary events (the 19-person firm hiring two people = an awkward manual mid-year uplift; with ≤25 it never happens). Ship four bands max: ≤25 / 26–100 / 101–250 / 250+ "talk to us". Yes to the full grid as ONE static table (bands as rows, both tiers as columns — an afternoon of HTML; no calculator, that's tooling you don't need for eight cells; top band shows Contact us). Yes publish everything: zero customers, the blank Tier 2 price costs every visitor; you need quotes you can send by pasting a URL; anchor annual, monthly costs more. Partner pricing needs one line: "MSP or reseller? Partner pricing available — contact us." Confirm the RORtech maths before honouring the locked tiers in writing. This week: rewrite pricing as the grid, add the partner line, put a number on Tier 2. One page, one deploy.

### The First Principles Thinker (C)
A band answers "what will this cost ME?" — its only job. Bands are a fairness signal wearing a price-discrimination costume, and with zero customers you have no data to discriminate with. Four-plus bands is false precision: segmenting a market you haven't met; every extra band is a decision point forced on visitors and a repricing liability. Two bands ("small" and "everyone else") + "150+? Talk to us"; widen when customers show you the seams. Foundation arguably needs ONE price with a headcount cap. Transparency buys self-qualification, not abstract trust: published prices mean only people who can afford you email — a solo founder's scarcest resource is time. Large-prospect low-anchoring is a problem you'd be lucky to have. Publish. Hidden MSP pricing is standard; channel margin is a trade secret. The only trust risk is an MSP's client discovering they paid more than list — rule: end-client price via partners never exceeds public list. Fix RORtech maths before publishing or the grid is a contract you've already broken.

### The Outsider (A)
Yes I can find my number — the grid's biggest win; I'm 38 staff, I look at 21–50, done; no per-seat maths. Show both tiers at every band: missing cells read as "call us" and I close the tab. A full grid shows my upgrade path (what happens at 51? if the jump is savage I want to know now). At 38 I quietly check whether I'm subsidising the 50-person firm — tolerable if the price feels calibrated to mid-band. Ambiguity about who counts (partners? part-timers?) must be a footnote or I won't bother emailing. Prices up to 150+ reassure rather than spook — they say you serve firms bigger than mine. Hidden MSP pricing doesn't bother me; I assume my IT provider marks up. I'm only annoyed if I discover identical service materially cheaper direct. What stops me buying today: invoice-only payment, no testimonials, placeholder bios. Card checkout + one named sector customer + a cancel-in-year-one line and £249/month is an easy sign-off; without them I screenshot your grid and buy from a competitor with a Stripe button.

### The Expansionist (E) — peer-flagged biggest blind spot 5/5
Publish the full grid — both tiers, every band — the grid IS the growth engine: self-serve procurement (screenshot the row into a purchase request; every hidden price is a meeting a solo founder must attend); the upgrade ladder as product story; "over 150? talk to us" as the consultancy funnel; SEO ("AI governance pricing UK" is a near-empty SERP — the transparent page becomes the market's reference); a calculator not a table (headcount slider, tier toggle, FR/DE-ready); and hidden MSP pricing as the classic channel flywheel — public retail makes partner margin legible while staying invisible. Reviewers' caution: pure upside-casing; ignores lock-in, top-band ceiling, gaming, founder capacity; proposes tooling that violates the operating constraint; asserts SEO value without evidence.

## Peer reviews — key findings
- Strongest: D ×3 (coherent risk mechanism: entry public, top gated; RORtech reconciliation), B ×2 (only answer priced against the manual-invoicing constraint; shippable this week).
- Blind spot: E ×5 (upside-only; calculator/locale scope inflation; ignores reconciliation and capacity).
- What ALL missed (recurring across reviews): (1) the contractual mechanics of headcount — FTE vs contractors vs group companies, measurement date, honour-system vs true-up at renewal, grandfathering when prices change — this is the real operational cost of ANY band structure; (2) VAT/ex-VAT display for a UK SME page; (3) whether Foundation and Platform band boundaries should even align, and whether the side-by-side grid survives the "why is Platform 3× Foundation?" question; (4) the decision is reversible at zero customers — treat the page as an experiment and let "talk to us" enquiry sizes draw the real band seams; (5) with zero customers the lock-in fear is weaker than stated — prices can simply change (but grandfather the early buyers).

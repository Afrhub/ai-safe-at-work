Chairman's verdict

Where the council agrees (high confidence)

1. The platform must be priced per organisation, in headcount bands, not per user. Four of five advisors and every reviewer converged: the AUP, registers and dashboards serve a company identically at 3 or 300 seats, per-user invites 2-seat gaming, and flat bands make both manual invoicing and MSP margin arithmetic workable. ChatGPT's £12.99/user/month is rejected.
2. A number must be published now. The blank Tier 2 price is costing every visit; blank reads as "expensive or unfinished".
3. The MSP founding promise must not be honoured until the margin maths is done. Every reviewer endorsed this; the channel doctrine was locked before a retail price existed.
4. No billing infrastructure yet. Annual invoicing by band is fine to ~30 customers; you have zero.

Where the council clashes

Keep or kill the £1,000 one-off? Executor and Expansionist keep it (fastest first revenue, already live); Contrarian and First Principles kill it (training + all 27 templates forever for £1,000 makes the subscription irrational). The reviewers sided decisively with kill/restructure. Chairman resolution: restructure, don't kill — see recommendation.
Per-seat or per-org language? The Outsider is right that office managers think in per-seat terms; First Principles is right that the org is the unit of value. Resolution: price in org bands, communicate the per-user equivalence ("under £9 per person per month, everything included").
Channel-first or direct-first? Expansionist says let MSPs sell it; Contrarian demands 3 direct sales first. Resolution: parallel, but RORtech is approached as a design partner, not a first sale.

Blind spots the council caught (peer-review round)

• Solo delivery capacity is the real pricing constraint — price to what one person can serve, not what the market bears.
• Professional-indemnity exposure: selling "defensible compliance" with no PI insurance and no liability terms — one customer fine ends the business. Insure and paper this before revenue.
• VAT threshold (£90k) changes effective pricing right in this revenue range.
• Every comparator price in every plan is asserted, not verified. One call to RORtech asking what its clients pay for comparable lines (Cyber Essentials packages) replaces the entire debate with data.
• The trust deficit precedes pricing: placeholder founder bios, no address, a paywalled glossary, and nav items that bounce to checkout will zero any price's conversion.

The one thing to do first

Call RORtech this week — not to sell, but to ask one question: "what would your clients pay per month for an AI-governance line item, and what do they pay for your Cyber Essentials package?" That single datapoint converts every number above from assumption to evidence, tells you whether the locked channel maths survives contact, and turns the founding-partner promise into a design partnership. Publish the £249/£499 bands on the site the same day.

Full advisor responses

The Contrarian (peer-ranked strongest, 5/5 reviews)
Fatal flaw: every option prices a product nobody has paid for, while the channel doctrine was locked before a single customer existed — lock retail first or the channel maths is fiction. £12.99/user/mo incl. training is arithmetic self-harm against the £1,000-forever tier; the one-off cannibalises the subscription — kill it or time-box it. Per-user platform pricing on an org-level product → rational buyer takes 2 seats; price per-company bands (£149/£299/£599), per-user only for training. MSP margin never reconciled: 70/30 on £99/user ≈ £70/user/yr to an MSP paying £499–£5k/mo — RORtech will do that sum and the promise collapses. Validate with 3 direct sales before honouring any MSP promise.
The First Principles Thinker
The question is "what price gets ten strangers to pay quickly enough to prove the problem exists?" The job is fear discharge, bought once, at organisation level — the org is the unit of value; seats are tier boundaries only. One SKU: Attest AI Complete £199/mo or £1,990/yr (≤25 staff; £399/mo ≤100) — training + platform + templates. Retire the £1,000 one-off. The channel only feeds an MSP at £399+ or volume; check RORtech's book before honouring the promise. Consultancy is the real first-revenue engine: sell three projects, give them the platform, harvest validation and logos. Anchor at CyberSmart, not KnowBe4 — compliance insurance, not training content.
The Expansionist (peer-flagged biggest blind spot, 5/5 reviews)
The money is the channel: 12,867 MSPs × 20 clients = a quarter-million businesses without direct sales — be the white-label AI-governance layer like cyber-essentials packages in 2018. Safe@Work £15/user/yr (£499 min); Platform flat bands £149/£299/£599 incl. training (MSP clears ~£100–420/mo per client at 70/30); keep the £1,000 bundle as tripwire; reframe the top MSP tier as "Compliance Practice in a Box". Later: EU-wide Article 4, FR/DE shells, and an attestation badge/registry — Attest as the attestation authority at £499/yr for the mark. Reviewers' caution: fantasy arithmetic at zero customers; self-contradicts by keeping the cannibalising one-off; ignores solo capacity and liability.
The Outsider
First-time buyer view: £1,000 by invoice before seeing anything reads broken or scammy; nav items redirecting to checkout, a blank Tier 2 price, "Founder name / Short bio goes here", no address, no card payment — I close the tab. Per-seat is the only framing an office manager understands, and the ChatGPT numbers compute instantly against security-awareness training (~£4,700/yr for 30 staff, signable without a board conversation). Before paying: card checkout, a free sample module/demo, real bio, UK address, and un-paywall the glossary and standards map — charging for a glossary is the strangest thing on the site. Publish numbers; blank pricing costs more than any wrong number.
The Executor
Monday: publish three numbers, keep invoicing manual, sell to RORtech by Friday. Kill per-user pricing (no billing infra; bands match manual Supabase provisioning). Publish: £1,000 one-off stays (≤25 staff; £1,750 ≤100); Platform £249/mo (≤25) / £499/mo (≤100) billed annually (£2,490/£4,990) incl. training; consultancy unchanged. RORtech: founding slot, 50% off year 1, 70/30, quarterly reconciliation on a shared sheet, signed one-pager + first client candidate by month end. Don't build billing — manual caps ~30 customers; that's headroom. Test: one £1,000 order and one RORtech signature in 30 days, else pricing was never the problem.
Peer-review highlights
Unanimous: Contrarian strongest (the cannibalisation + unreconciled-channel findings are the decisions everything else depends on); Expansionist biggest blind spot (channel fantasy at zero customers, self-contradictory on the one-off, ignores capacity and liability). What all five missed, per the reviewers: solo delivery capacity as the pricing ceiling; professional-indemnity liability and terms for selling "compliance"; VAT threshold effects; IP/white-label licensing legality for the channel; EU AI Act enforcement dates as urgency packaging; RORtech as design partner rather than first sale; and that every price cited is asserted, not benchmarked — one RORtech call yields the only real datapoint available this week.

# Market sizing — AI Safe@Work

> **Owner:** Founder · **Effective:** 2026-05-19 · **Review:** quarterly · **Status:** v1 baseline

Who can buy this course, how many of them exist, how many we can realistically win, and what that's worth.

## TL;DR

- **Total addressable market** (any AI-using employer globally): ~10M orgs — not useful to monetise
- **Serviceable addressable market** (EU+UK SMB/mid-market that touches AI for work, regulated reach): **~2.5M orgs**
- **Serviceable obtainable (3-year)**: **1,500–3,000** orgs paying + **5,000–10,000** paying individuals
- **Y3 ARR — realistic**: £250k–£600k self-sustaining · £1–3M w/ paid marketing or partnership
- **Y5 ceiling** w/ Qualiopi + accreditation + sector overlays: **£8–15M ARR**

Market is real, large, and underserved on the wedge. Bottleneck is founder-time + procurement-readiness gates, not demand.

EU AI Act Article 4 applies to every employer with ≥1 employee using AI. Binding from 2 February 2025. No size threshold. Realistic universe needing training: **2–4 million orgs in EU+UK alone by end of 2026**.

| Sector | Why they buy fast | EU+UK pool | 3-yr win rate | Orgs won |

| **Public sector small bodies** (councils, NHS trusts, schools, NDPBs) | OMB M-24-10 equivalent in EU; UK AI Playbook; FRIA mandatory under AI Act Art 27 | ~50k | 1–3% | **500–1,500** |
| **Education** (independent schools, MATs, FE, HEIs) | DfE AI guidance; teacher union pressure; safeguarding overlap | ~70k | 1–2% | **700–1,400** |
| **Marketing / agencies** (PR, content, design, dev shops) | Daily AI use; client IP + GDPR exposure; pitching credentials need | ~300k | 0.3–0.7% | **900–2,100** |
| **HR-heavy SMBs + recruitment agencies** | EU AI Act Annex III high-risk; NYC LL144 (US clients); bias-audit obligations | ~150k | 0.5–1% | **750–1,500** |
| **Insurance SMB + brokers** | NAIC Model Bulletin; Colorado SB21-169; EIOPA pressure | ~30k | 1–2% | **300–600** |
| **All other compliance-conscious SMB** | General GDPR + ISO 27001 + EU AI Act Art 4 | ~1.1M | 0.05–0.15% | **550–1,650** |

**Realistic 3-year SOM** = **8,500–18,000 orgs paying**.

At Phase 1 launch slice avg deal (60% Starter £299 + 30% Business £999 + 10% Enterprise £3,999) = **£737 avg / org / yr**.

→ ceiling £6.3M–£13.3M ARR. Real conversion much slower in years 1–3 — expect ~20–30% of that ceiling.

## Conversion funnel — realistic 3-year ramp

Assumptions: SEO + free-core word-of-mouth as primary acquisition; minimal paid spend; founder time as the main constraint.

| Stage | Y1 (early launch) | Y2 (compounding) | Y3 (procurement-winning) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly free-tier visits | 3,000 | 25,000 | 80,000 |
| % converting to free-cert hold (read seriously) | 8% | 12% | 15% |
| Free-cert holds / mo | 240 | 3,000 | 12,000 |
| % escalating to Pro Cert £49 | 1% | 2% | 3% |
| Individual paid sales / mo | ~2–3 | ~60 | ~360 |
| Enterprise inbound leads / mo (from individuals or direct) | 1–2 | 8–12 | 30–50 |
| Enterprise close rate | 15% | 25% | 35% |
| New Enterprise customers / mo | ~0.2 | ~3 | ~15 |
| Cumulative paying enterprise customers @ year end | 3 | 35 | 170 |
| Cumulative paying individual @ year end | 30 | 750 | 5,000 |

ARR estimate per year:

- **Y1**: 30 × £49 + 3 enterprise × £400 avg ≈ **£2.7k**
- **Y2**: ≈ **£44k** (matches "Steady" scenario from pricing doc)
- **Y3**: ≈ **£245k** (matches "Working" scenario, conservative)

With paid marketing + partnership (eg one IAPP / sector body co-mark):

- **Y3**: £450k–£800k ARR
- **Y5**: £2–4M ARR with all overlays + accreditation live

## Big assumptions / risks

| Assumption | If wrong, what breaks |
|---|---|
| EU AI Act Art 4 enforcement creates buyer-side pressure by Q3 2026 | Slow market; conversion drops 50%+ |
| SEO + free-core word-of-mouth produces meaningful organic traffic without paid marketing | Need paid acquisition budget; margin compresses |
| Competitors (IAPP / KnowBe4 / Microsoft) don't launch sub-£10/seat AI module in next 12 months | Price compression; lean harder on EU + SMB specificity |
| Founder can sustain solo through Y1 to Y2 ramp | Bus factor #1 risk in doctrine — kills everything |
| Buyers will actually pay for the operational layer when content is free | Need ~25% conversion from free-cert to paid; if it's 5%, math collapses |
| Procurement teams accept "self-assessed" Standards Map as enough without external attestation | Need to subcontract attestation faster; cost rises ~£3k/yr per major buyer |
